Trixie Lulamoon: Weekend Magician

Episode 01 (Pilot): Magic Act of Treachery




	




The life of Trixie Lulamoon hasn't ever been glamorous. She's tried so many things, so many times, that she's honestly forgotten all of the dead end side jobs she's tried to do. Everything from cashier to costume designer, it's blown up in her face.

Two things have remained constant, however, in the last 31 years. First, her best friend and ex-on-again-off-again boyfriend Dusk Seeker. Second, the inevitable typhoon she creates in his life, and has, since they were teenagers.

Now, however, Dusk was finally ready to settle down. His life was evolving. Change was coming. He was engaged to Rarity Belle, the fashion designer. Someone that came into their lives in High School, when she transferred to Ponyville High from Whinnysota. Dusk had long had an interest in her, and for her part, Rarity had accepted the.. arrangement.. dating.. and break-ups of Dusk and Trixie, as they tried to make things work.

It didn't help that Dusk's father didn't completely agree with his idea to join the Royal Guard, but.. it is what it is. They try to make the best of it.


December 17, 1011 C.S.
7:02AM Canterlot Royal Time

Dusk was exhausted. Three days of tactical training for the upcoming winter holiday events. With Griffon terrorists making waves in the news, the guard commanders thought it best to make sure all the Guard Teams had extra training on every possible alternative, to keep them sharp.

He got to the door of his apartment, and fished out his keys.. but he could hear.. his TV going?!

Oh, not this again.

He unlocked the door, and just trudged in, taking off his tactical gear as he did. He knew what was up as soon as he smelled his espresso machine (that Rarity got him) having been run.. and.. not immediately cleaned.

There she was, on the couch, in slightly faded pink pajama pants a size too large for her (again, as she's clearly lost weight), and a white tank top.

Trixie.

Dusk: "..Trixie, you can't keep doing this, I'm engaged, now." He shrugged, setting his tactical vest on the rack. "Look, Trixie-"

Trixie: "It's just a few days. I'm starting a new job, Dusk, come on.. please?" She gave him the pouty eyes, as she finished chewing the last of his Biscotti.

Dusk: "Ugh.." He slumped forwards a bit. "Alright, alright, you know where the couch is. And no more buying new series on Hayflix." He wagged his finger. "I may be getting a promotion, but I want the bonus to go towards the wedding."

Trixie: "YAY!" She jumped up and hugged him around the neck. "Trixie knew you would understand!"

The time they'd spent dating all those years ago, and going even further back, the time they'd spent growing up together had taught Dusk one thing: This wasn't going to be a few days, and he was going to have "Sex and the City of Canterlot" on his account.. again.

That and he'd have to stock up on snacks.

Dusk: "So, what's the job this time?" He asked, digging into the fridge for a Kirin beer.. and finding that she'd not gotten into those, yet.

Trixie: "Ah.." She smiled a bit. "..I'm getting a gig as a stage magician."

Trixie: "Trixie does not care." She flipped her hair a bit. "Also, I stole your towels."

Dusk: "..I'll get the extras.." He cracked the beer open.

Trixie: "It's a real chance, Dusk." She stopped and looked down at her empty espresso cup. "Okay, the pay sucks, but.." She shrugged. "..it's something."

Dusk: "And what'll you be doing? Where's the stage?" He sipped the beer as he unzipped his coveralls.

Trixie: "Well, a-" She had to pause, seeing his.. well, impressive muscled frame.. and remind herself that she threw this away ages ago and was not entitled to so much as a fleeting thought. "-ctually, I'll be.. you know.. a birthday party and event magician." She had to blink a few times to keep herself from staring.

Dusk: "Oh." He shrugged, and chugged half the beer, to keep HIMSELF from staring, now, seeing her tank top dangle a bit too loosely from her frame, and reminding himself that he was, in fact, engaged. "Still, ah.." He shrugged. "..got a few gigs lined up?"

Trixie: "Mhm." She gave an almost cat-like saunter around a few steps. "The talent agency has me lined up for the Senators' Charity Dinner tomorrow evening."

Dusk: "Oh, good. I can keep an eye on you, then." He smirked.

They both stared at each other, blank-faced, knowing that what he just said had started far too many nights of regrets.. well, the regrets came in the morning, actually.. the nights were just sweaty.

Trixie: "Oh?" She shrugged. "Well, at least I'll be well protected.." She stopped, and blushed. "..we need to be.."

Dusk: "I'm taking a shower and sleeping." He stated firmly. "No joining me."

Trixie: "O-Of course." Not that she wasn't thinking about it.. again..

So, Dusk went and showered. It was a hot shower at first, but his mind started wandering disloyal. So, he shut the hot water off after he washed his hair and stood there. Reminding himself of the last time. And the time before that.

Really, almost every time.

The breakups had never been terribly bad, but they had been because of her, every time. She wanted things, he wanted things, they'd wanted each other, but in the end, Trixie was too much of a free spirit, and Dusk - despite the fallout with his father - wanted something more traditional.

In the end, Trixie never really grew up.

She still had fantasies of this and that. Desires. Wants. Needs. She wanted to be rich and famous, but be darned if she couldn't string things together well enough to even make a living. Every time, every loss, she came back to him.

He slid into his bed and rested himself. Stretched out. A big part of him felt guilt at his thoughts. His mind wanting that one thing he'd had that felt real - the time he'd spent with her, on and off. But.. now.. he had someone that would anchor his life.

Rarity.

When his thoughts went there, he smiled to himself. Unlike with Trixie, Rarity had been very strict about not frolicking before marriage. They were to be married in January. She had, though, made every effort to tease him and remind him what he was waiting for.

Sleep came. He knew he'd only get about six hours of it, as he promised to meet his lady love later on for a late lunch. Then, he had to report for duty before the Charity Dinner and go over plans, prepare equipment, and all that.
