Kingdom of Germaneigh: Warships (Konigliche Marine des Deutschland)



Germaneigh's navy isn't as impressive as the Ambleonian or Prench navies, and while they had a strong naval tradition, they never had nearly as many vessels as either nation. The Germane Fleet prided itself on strict discipline and dedication, and were usually regarded as to-a-pony more skilled at shipkeeping than the Ambleonian and Prench forces. Their warships rely on extremely heavy armor to last longer in a fight, and make use of hydraulics and steam power to reduce crewing requirements.
Because of Germaneigh's unique political structure, the initial Naval force was small, until the various Duchies that made up the United Kingdom of Germaneigh fully committed to the war effort. Once done, every shipbuilding facility in the nation was put to work, and the Germane Navy expanded greatly.
Germaneigh's ship prefix is "SMS" - or Seiner Majestat Schiff. They use a hull classification system more like Equestria's.

Nimble enough to "chase shell splashes," these Germane naval hot rods can, in an emergency, go to 125% engine output and exceed 41 knots in a short run.

Converted into aircraft carriers, the Thuringia-class holds 48 aircraft, putting it on par with most other NEIGHTO carriers. Owing to its width, and the design of the Messerschmidt planes, the Thuringia-class is extremely efficient in its aircraft stowage. It is intended that the carriers operate in pairs, with the third carrier in reserve at home. It has a decent anti-aircraft capability, but relies more on coverage from the rest of the fleet. With the new engines, and a good bit of armor removed (including the entire internal citadel - replaced by an armored flight deck instead), its weight has dropped considerably, and it has a much higher speed as a result.

The most massive warship afloat, the H-Klasse mounts powerful 17-inch guns, a massive amount of dual-purpose guns, the powerful and long ranged 47mm Skoda anti-aircraft cannon, and loads of 11mm machine guns for close range defense, with a full squadron of Scout-Fighter planes, and a full Regiment of Marinekampftruppen. This gigantic tub is fully proofed to its own guns, with the citadel spanning from before the forward magazine, aft past the aft magazine, and covering the machine gears all the way to the ends. Sinking something this huge would take an incredible amount of ordnance, especially with its anti-torpedo bulges and double-bottom hull. Its guns are exceptionally high velocity for its caliber, and can easily overmatch anything in Tirek's naval arsenal. The high explosive payload is also impressive, capable of launching a 1361kg (3,000lbs) shell at a velocity of almost 840 m/s (2,750fps), to a distance of up to 66km with a rocket assisted projectile. The "H-Klasse" finally gives 'the good guys' the bigger stick, for once.

The Prague-class Light Cruisers are designed for torpedo attacks, anti-submarine work, and to fend off enemy cruisers. The long barrel, high velocity 6.3-inch guns pack more armor-piercing power than the Equestrian 152mm guns, and can penetrate almost as well as the Roaman 7.5-inch guns. The torpedo compliment makes it a serious threat to any enemy ship its guns can't handle. Unlike the Rotenburg-class Heavy Cruisers, it does not have a rocket-assisted HE shell for shore bombardment, but it does have "dirty" HE shells intended to start fires, packed with a slower burning explosive mixture.

With so many Heimdall-class Destroyers, Germaneigh decided to modify them for flexible duty - either defending the home shores, or participating in fleet actions with the Hochseeflotte ("High Seas Fleet"). They are, however, not the Germane High Command's first choice for this duty. Modifications include removing two of the 15cm guns and replacing them with 12.8cm dual-purpose guns, refining the torpedo armament, adding anti-submarine protection, and replacing the original 11mm machine guns with 20mm autocannon. Germaneigh's move to 20mm autocannons instead of 11mm machine guns based on the EM-4 Browning reflects a greater caution against flying threats, and a desire for more "punch" against extremely small vessels intended for possible suicide missions.

The follow-on Odin-class Zerstorers are simply improved Heimdall-class designs, with much the same armament and a new layout. The smokestacks are changed to a different design, but in all respects, it's extremely similar. The difference is, Odin-class Zerstorers have sonar - Heimdall-class vessels don't, and rely on other vessels to spot submarines for them. This extra capability makes them preferred for Fleet duties, or to act as "destroyer flotilla leaders" to the slightly smaller Heimdall-class vessels.

These smaller vessels are designed specifically to guard the Capital Ships of the Germane fleet from both aircraft and submarines, as well as small vessels of around its size and smaller (like Patrol-Torpedo Boats), from close range attack. They have sonar to detect subs, and a good AAA suite to defend against aircraft. When forced to engage larger vessels, it does have torpedoes, and its 12.8cm DP gun is as effective as the Equestrian 5-inch gun against warships (though notably better against aircraft).
