Collective Khyangese Republic: Firearms



The CKRCS has institutes that develop industrial sites for production of goods. These institutes first study the subject at hand, and develop all needs for it, in house. The story of Khyangese firearms starts with the "Fire Stick" first seen over 70 years ago, in their conflict with Prance. Essentially a small blast cup with a dart in it, at the end of a long stick, these "Fire Sticks" were often just as dangerous to the user, and were smartly outranged by Prench Crossbowponies. Their foray into "modern" firearms began with espionage against Equestria as early as the start of the war. The first rifle developed, the Type 1, proved to be a general failure. The Herdiet Union developed far superior weapons to the Type 1, so, again, the Khyangese simply stole it, adjusted a few things, and fielded Herdiet-based designs instead of trying to develop their own.


Type 1 Fire Rifle
WEAPON TYPE: Battle Rifle
CALIBER: 8x38mm Type 1
CAPACITY: 26 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 200 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 340mm/13.38 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 820mm/32.3 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 246mm/9.68 inches
TOTAL MASS: 10lbs/4.535kg
The Type 1 "Fire Rifle" was fielded only once - the Battle of Chowsin Ridge. 137 examples and 212 rounds of ammunition were collected from the battlefield. An analysis by General K concluded the weapon was many times overbuilt, and while the parts were inceredibly robust, they were oftentimes far thicker, wider, or longer than they needed to be. The barrels had rifling that was cut far too shallow to permit tight engagement from the bullet, and the bullets were unjacketed soft steel, that would wear out the barrels quickly. Even the cases were a type of lacquered steel, and as they didn't remove heat from the chamber effectively, the weapon was prone to overheating after extended use on full automatic. It's very clear the weapon was based on very early Patton and late Sherman design concepts, and ended up looking like a misshapen AKM-type rifle. The Khyangese military would later simply copy Herdiet designs. Even the caliber was dropped from production.
