Principality of Vulpesylvania: Warships (Vulpine Royal Navy)



Vulpesylvania, much like their geograpic mirror - Greece - in the human world, had a very, very robust sailing and naval warfare tradition.. that fell shorter and shorter as the world modernized around them, and they suffered indignations at the hands of some of their neighbors. Before ther war, they had a decent sized fleet of very small vessels (25-40 meters) armed with ballistae and similar weapons, all wooden, all sail-rigged. While this gave them something to use, and was reltively on par with their neighbors, they lacked any powered vessels at all - whereas the Ambleonian Royal Navy had several, and Equestria's merchant ships were about 10% coal powered, with coal bunkerage a non-issue as the ships remained close to the coasts.
Vulpesylvania's ship prefix is "Vulpine Majesty's Ship" or VMS. They adopted hull codes matching Equestria's.

On the advice of General K, one of the Prince Orlando-class Battelcruisers was converted into a Light Aircraft Carrier while under construction. Smaller than the Canidan Mount Yakutat class, the Mount Athos class has two units - the Mount Athos being converted from the planned 4th Battlecruiser, and the Mount Olympus built intentionally. The shorter and thinner Vulpine carriers have a smaller aircraft compliment, but can add additional units with deck parking. Working in tandem, the two units can maintain a mission pace faster than one carrier alone. Unique to the Vulpine carriers is the mounting of the superstructure and smoke stacks on the side of the hull, so the flight and hangar decks are completely uninterrupted.

Thin and almost spritely, the Prince Orlando-class Battlecruisers share the basic hull shape of the Thuringria-class battleships, but with more room for engines, better engines, and a refined layout. The thinner hull and redesigned lower bottom allows it to achieve speeds on par with heavier Scout Cruisers. With its 10-inch guns, it has enough striking power to take down any Cruiser-weight opponent, and with its relatively high speed, the ability to run away from any Battleship-weight one, if its high velocity 10-inch AP shells can't pierce its hide. Like the Canidan 5-inch guns, the Vulpine 5-inch guns can be used for AAA as well as anti-ship. For ease of production, its machine guns are Polelish UKM-1 11mm's, despite Vulpesylvania producing its own 11mm heavy machine gun.

Vulpesylvania decided on a VERY light "Light Cruiser" for their fleet. Depending entirely on rapid fire and high shell output to inflict damage, the 5" (127mm) guns can also function as anti-aircraft weapons, thus preparing for the use of Aircraft of some kind, by enemy forces. With 20 available guns, the "weight of shell" for the cruisers is immense, though their total range is rather short, at just 16km. By comparison, the 10" (254mm) guns of the Battlecruisers have a range of 32km.

The torpedo boats of the Vulpine fleet, the Athens class destroyers can put out 12 torpedoes in rapid succession, and a blistering 10 barrels of 5-inch gunfire to boot. Very fast as far as Destroyer-sized vessels go, they dovetail nicely into the torpedo striking forces of the NEIGHTO fleet, and act as spoilers to an enemy battle line, with their fast and deep strikes, while the Cruisers line up for gun battles.
