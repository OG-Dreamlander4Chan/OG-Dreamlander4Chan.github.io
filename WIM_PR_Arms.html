Kingdom of Prance: Firearms



Prance siezed upon the concept of arms production faster and with more gusto than Ambleion or Germaneigh, directly requesting the aid of General K in creating a unique weapon design that they felt offered them an advantage. In general, several Prench companies and national arsenals jumped into the fray. Among them was the Industrial Arms Society of St. Chamond, headed by Professor LeFaucheux, a highly respected engineer and inventor, the Saint Chamond and Saint Etienne national armories, and the Chatellerault Armaments Plant. Together, and in very Prench fashion, they developed their own arms, while also handling arsenal work of donated firearms from Equestria. The 5,000 XER-3 Patton Rifles ordered and partially assembled were largely split between Prance (1,934) and Germaneigh (2,117), with the remainder donated to Neighpon (949). Prance directly adapted the Patton for the 7mm NEIGHTO cartridge.

The Modéle 1002 is a Patton given as War Aid to Prance that has been rechambered to 7mm NEIGHTO, and intended to arm the Prench Foreign Legion only. The conversion was designed by Professor LeFaucheux himself, to be as cost effective and simple as possible.

Unqiue is the order of the day with Prench firearms. The FP-2 replaces the initially sent "Sniper Sherman" rifles in 7mm NEIGHTO (designated FP-1), but uses the 9mm Equestrian caliber, making Prance the only other NEIGHTO nation to do so as of 1005 C.S. Like the Canidan Timberwolf, the FP-2 is a bolt action weapon, but a straight-pull. This means that the bolt handle is pulled straight back after each shot. The FP-2 has an automatic bolt return, meaning it does not have to be guided forwards, and the bolt can be locked with a tab just ahead of the bolt handle. For simplicity, the FP-2 was designed to use "Super Sherman" magazines.

OVERALL LENGTH: 950mm/37.4 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 252mm/9.92 inches
TOTAL MASS: 16lbs/7.25kg
Simplicity and light weight were the design intentions behind the AA-1. A modified version with a shorter barrel was developed as a short-lived "fighter harness" for Pegasi, but it was neither fielded, nor extensively produced, before proper aircraft became available. The AA-1 has a longer barrel than most other machine guns of its class, and is fairly long overall, but it does have a folding stock, making transport easier. Its stability and tendency to resist overheating, however, makes up for this shortcoming.
