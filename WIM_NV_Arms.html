Kingdom of Navarre: Firearms



The Navarese freely admitted to spying on Equestria to obtain the knowledge and information needed to build firearms. During the Navarese Civil War, they manufactured arms in smaller, less controlled settings in a lot of cases. While the majority of arms produced were, in fact, Zhukovs chambered in 9x18mm (as somewhat intended of the design), they also produced their own Assault Rifle and Pistol designs, as well as myriad other arms. However, one limitation is that the initially-designed Navarese arms did not use powders as high of pressure as Equestrian and NEIGHTO arms of the same calibers. As a result, NEIGHTO ammo cannot be used in certain Navarese arms, as they will cause a chamber rupture.

An odd linguistic note, the Navarese use the term "Combate Brazo" (lit. Combat Arm - as in an appendage), as they initially misunderstood what "Fire Arm" quite meant. So, to them, weapons are appendages, or an extension of one's foreleg.


Combate Brazo-1 "Star Pistola" (CB-1)
WEAPON TYPE: Double Action Semi-Automatic Pistol
MANUFACTURER: Star Bonifacio Echieverria S.A.
CALIBER: 9x23mm Navarese
CAPACITY: 7 + 1 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 40 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 104mm/4 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 194mm/7.63 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 136mm/5.35 inches
TOTAL MASS: 1.8lbs/0.82kg
The Star Pistol is based on the Morgan design, but smaller. It's chambered in 9x23mm, but CANNOT use NEIGHTO ammo, as it will damage the weapon fairly quickly. The 9x23mm Navarese round only reaches a velocity of 290m/s (compared to 370m/s for 9x23mm NEIGHTO), giving it a mere 355 joules of muzzle energy.
Combate Brazo-2 "Fusil de Asalto" (CB-2)
WEAPON TYPE: Assault Rifle
MANUFACTURER: Astra-Unceta y Cia
CALIBER: 7x40mm Navarese
CAPACITY: 30 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 300 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 360mm/14.17 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 860mm/33.85 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 248mm/8.1 inches
TOTAL MASS: 7.1lbs/3.2kg
The CB-2 Rifle is a fascinating fusion of the Patton and Sherman designs, and while the 7mm cartridge isn't up to the same snuff as the 7mm NEIGHTO round, it is pretty accurate within its range envelope.
