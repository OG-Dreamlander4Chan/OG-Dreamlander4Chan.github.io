Kingdom of Navarre: Firearms



The Navarese freely admitted to spying on Equestria to obtain the knowledge and information needed to build firearms. During the Navarese Civil War, they manufactured arms in smaller, less controlled settings in a lot of cases. While the majority of arms produced were, in fact, Zhukovs chambered in 9x18mm (as somewhat intended of the design), they also produced their own Assault Rifle and Pistol designs, as well as myriad other arms. However, one limitation is that the initially-designed Navarese arms did not use powders as high of pressure as Equestrian and NEIGHTO arms of the same calibers. As a result, NEIGHTO ammo cannot be used in certain Navarese arms, as they will cause a chamber rupture.

An odd linguistic note, the Navarese use the term "Combate Brazo" (lit. Combat Arm - as in an appendage), as they initially misunderstood what "Fire Arm" quite meant. So, to them, weapons are appendages, or an extension of one's foreleg.

The Star Pistol is based on the Morgan design, but smaller. It's chambered in 9x23mm, but CANNOT use NEIGHTO ammo, as it will damage the weapon fairly quickly. The 9x23mm Navarese round only reaches a velocity of 290m/s (compared to 370m/s for 9x23mm NEIGHTO), giving it a mere 355 joules of muzzle energy.

9x23mm NEIGHTO (Post-War)

The "Restorer" Carbines were made from donated Shermans and some were scratch-built to the Sherman layout and design during the Civil War. Afterwards, in a bid to use them as best as possible, all were rearsenalled to accept 9x23mm NEIGHTO standard pressure ammo. Those in fully automatic were given to the Policía Real, while those only capable of semi-automatic fire were given to the Guarda Civil as reserve and training weapons. Since some were made locally, there is some variation in design and looks. The image here shows a post-Civil War, Government-manufactured Policía version. The Carbines were named for the general intent of the Royalist faction - to restore the Royal throne.

Navarre's SMG, the Z-4/5 is pretty standard as far as NEIGHTO SMG's go, though like the Roamans, it's largely milled and not stamped, with care taken in each component, to assure a very high quality fit and finish. It's a bit larger than other NEIGHTO SMG's due to the recoil system used. The Star Z-4 prototype SMG was outwardly similar to the final product - this, the Z-4/5 - however, it had a fixed stock that was found to be cumbersome in close quarters, so, all production Z-4/5's were made with folding stocks.

The CETME Model "L" ("Lancero") is intended to be "the Lance that stakes Tirek's heart" according to King Condor-Alfonso I. With Navarre joining NEIGHTO, they needed a rifle of their own, so they adapted the "Grant" series from Equestria, and made it a bit more suitable to Goats. The barrel was lengthened slightly to allow for the use of Rifle Greandes, and the bolt system was changed to a simple retention button on the receiver. Other than that, they're Grants, and can be treated as same.
