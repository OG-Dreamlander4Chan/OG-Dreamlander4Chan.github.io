Part 1: Iberian Lock-Step

TIME: 10:27 IT
DATE: DATE: War Day 732/6 Quartus 1004 C.S.
LOCATION: Don Francesco's Estate, Girul, Navarre

K: "How is Tirek involved?" I got right to the point.

Starlight: "Moreover.. if we help you drive him from your lands.. would that mean we can guarantee your support in NEIGHGTO?" She tag-teamed with me.

Duke Condor: "To the second question.. that will depend on how much of a nation, a people, I have remaining when the war ends.. and whether I become King." He gestured with a hoof. "Certainly, if everything goes well, I will be grateful, and offer whatever assistance I can." He then looked to me. "As to the first? Well.."

Duchess Condor: "Well." She came in with three maids, with hand carts, all with food on them. "That can wait for after you eat! Talk war on an empty stomach, again, mi amore, you get heartburn." She coo'd to him and gave him a small nuzzle.

K: "Yes, Ma'am." I nodded deeply to her.

Applejack: "Yeah, I could sure use a spot'a breakfast.." She blinked, as a vertiable feast was laid out before us.

Firecracker: "I see what you mean.." She leaned in to Gorcat. "..things do get strange around here."

Starlight: "I'm just happy we're friendly, here.." She saw all the food, and smiled broadly. "This looks amazing!"

I dug right in - it was a bit more than I expected. Cafe con Leche, a Bocadillos with cheese, Pan Con Tomate, Churros, and a Spanish potato omelette. I remembered that Spanish breakfasts were usually light, so, it was interesting to see three 'main courses' set on one plate. I surmised she guessed (correctly) that we hadn't had dinner the previous night.

Everyone ate with minimal conversation. I noted Gorcat was teaching Firecracker the ropes - he'd been with us for a long time, he knew how we operated. Dusty seemed very at-ease with these foods, and I guessed she'd had some before. Starlight and Rarity both ate dantily, though Rarity with more natural class, and Starlight more like someone that learned it later.

Applejack: "Ah'm sorry fer bein' so sore at'cha, hun.." She finally admitted, thumping her head into my right side. "..jes' - I was afraid you'd.. you know."

I leaned in and kissed her nose.

K: "It's okay, honey. I understood you were mad because you thought I'd put myself into too much danger, again. And, I'm the most likely one they'd've executed for.. say.. espionage or something." I shrugged, and kept eating. "This is fantasic, Duchess. I've never had all of these items together before."

Duchess Condor: "Ah! Then I shall have to see about treating you to more of our cuisine. It is.. such important part of who we are." She smiled.

Duke Condor: "I had once heard ponies prefer tea in the mornings.." He trailed off, seeing me drain the coffee cup quickly.

K: "I'm no pony, Your Highness." I said, addressing him more as 'king apparent' than as the Duke. "Humans drink coffee and tea both - I prefer coffee, but I do drink tea when it's available. We're omnivores, so, we literally eat anything that doesn't eat us first." I gave him a smile - no sense telling him I had goat once, and LOVED it.

Applejack: "An' I'll drink a cup'a whatever's available in the mornin' - sometimes, it's coffe, sometimes, tea." She smiled a bit.

Duke Condor: "Ah!" He smiled. "Would you care for more coffee them, Mariscal?"

K: "Please. Cafe solo." I didn't even need to hold up my cup - a maid came over with a decanter and poured it for me. "I recognize the Navarran preference for sweet in the morning, but I don't want to have too much sugar." I nodded slightly. "Not that it wasn't fantastic."

Duke Condor: "Ahaha! A gracious guest!" He bowed slightly to me. "I see you are a person I can respect."

Duchess Condor: "Si. He even speaks Navarese." She nodded to me.

Duke Condor: "Well, now that we have had our breakfast.." He said, as the maids removed empty plates - everyone had cleaned theirs. "We talk business." He nodded to me.

K: "Indeed." I nodded to my team. "Everyone here is an advisor on my General Staff, with each their own talents and inputs. Starlight, as an Ambassador, is an expert in negotiation." I said, validating their presence.

Duke Condor: "Even your wife?" He nodded to Applejack.

K: "Land Warfare Advsior, Logistics Advisor.." I nodded.

Duke Condor: "Aha!" He beamed a grin. "Is good to have such talented officers, good freinds, Amigos." He raised his coffee cup to each of them. "Now. Tirek." He lowered his head. "When this conflict started almost two years ago, we did not know exactly.. how.. certain things, certain events.. had come to pass." He looked up at me. "I have a confession I must make, Senor. Our government purchased information from a spy three years ago, about you and your designs for.. what you call fire arms." He enunicated that as two seperate words. "You may not understand this, but Ibexeria has had its own issues with Ponykind.. when we attempted to colonize the area known as Mulexico, we brought the Mules there our langauge and culture.. then, they reject our rule and throw us out - we know that Equestria assisted with this."

K: "I can't say anything about that - I've only been here for four years, sir. I don't know all of Equestria's history." I shrugged.

Duke Condor: "Is in the past, si?" He shook his head, and continued. "Our paranoia that Ponykind might seek our conquest or destruction has led us to make careful watch of Equestria, through middle men - professional lookers, si? Spies." He shrugged a tad. "When we find out about YOU? Ho-ho-ho.. there was some certain reservation! The old legend, you see? That a human would come, and help Tirek conquer the world, or destroy him. So, we watch, we watch to see if you are with Tirek or not. You make weapons.. we become a bit afraid. Our best engineers, chemists, and toymakers come together to take your design ideas, and make our own. So, if you are hostile, we have means to resist."

K: "Perfectly understandable, Duke Condor." I nodded to him. "If you're making an apology, there's no need - I would have done exactly the same in your position."

Duke Condor: "Ah!" He chuckled again. "See? A good guest!" He leaned forward and patted me on the shoulder. "You are a soldier, Mariscal - I thought you might understand." He grinned widely at me. "So. We start to have our own fire arms.. then, we have a political crisis. Certain elements in our government that have sympathy for the Herdiet Union? They win some seats in Cortes Generales. Enough to call for a referendum on the future of the Navarese Throne - they call for the King to be removed. They are voted down. The next thing we know, Colectivista Red Guard are raiding our capital.. many members of the Chamber of Deputies in Iruna.. they were arrested. Some members of the Senatos.. escaped." He shook his head. "Those arrested were executed. They also tried to assassinate King Alfonso the 8th, but instead killed his son, Crown Prince Alfonso Castile." He shrugged, heavy-hearted. "I am his father's brother.. was." He lowered his head. "My brother's marriage was supposed to unite the Castillan and Navarese cultures together at last. My nephew.. he was a Ibexian of the People, everyone loved him.."

K: "Interesting. In my world, your culture and Kingdom were conquered by the Castile and Aragonese.. oh.. about five hundred years before the last day I was on my Earth. I see, here, Navarese became the dominant culture." I nodded to him.

Duke Condor: "Not without great struggles. We, being Ibex, were very isolated for a long while. We raised up several major cultures - Asturias, Castile, Aragonese, and Navarese. Over time, and with trade, relations, marriages, and some war.. the Navarese and Aragonese became one, and the Asturians and Castile become the other. Once the crowns were reduced to two, then.. then the problems began. Conflict, hard feelings.. about a thousand years ago, the King of Castile and the King of Navarre met for a duel. Being evenly matched, it was no sure thing, but they threw aside the potential for bloodshed, neither wanting harm and strife to come to their nations' peoples. The King of Navarre narrowly won the duel.. and once he unified our lands under one Crown, each major faction was given a Principality - Navarre itself, then Aragon, Castile, and Asturias. Then, each major faction within was given a Duchy - Catalan, Valencia, Leon, Murcia, Toledo, Merida, Galicia, and Andalusia. While only a Navarran prince or princess could be king, the Royal Council consisted of the Dukes and Princes of the realm." He nodded, his eyes shut as he recited his history. "For long, the Castillians and the Navarese have had differences. To divide us would split us nearly in half. This marriage was cruicial." He sighed.

K: "Sounds like Tirek's style, to cut a wedge in a community just as they're trying to come together." I nodded. "How do the Herdiets play into this?"

Duke Condor: "They supported the Colectivistas. Mainly made up of disaffected lower class Castillians - Leonese and Galicians especially. This Civil War may have two main banners, but it is made up of everyone that has had disagreement with the last thousand years of history, each with their own flag. I myself represent my father's throne - the Carlian Family. My cousin represents the Alfonso Family, and their claim to the throne. Our king died of a broken heart six months after the struggle began, having lost everything. We have no clear claimant even to his throne - the Principality of Navarre itself. That is divided between the Carlians and the Alfonsos. Then, we have the Nationalists, who want no kings, but want a representative government only. We also have the National Syndicalists, who want a totalitarian form of government not unlike the Royal one, but with more representation and no patronomical inheritance of the role of leader. On the other side are the Colectivistas, but they also include the Galician Duchy's government that wants independence, and factions in the Valencian government who wish the same - they care less of how they are ruled, and more that they no longer wish to be in union with us." He used his table map to show us, roughly, where all this was - the map itself was pressed into the table as a cover pattern, and over a hundred years old.

K: "Like all Civil Wars, this is very messy." I nodded. "It's anything but Civil."

Duke Condor: "Si." He nodded slowly.

K: "And if NEIGHTO wants the support of your people, and your lands, we have to wade in and put a stop to the fighting. Somehow." I looked at Starlight. "Which isn't a purely military solution. I mean, it could be - we could simply inject enough military force into the region to kill everyone resisting that isn't on the side we choose to support. But, that would leave a LOT more dead. And that's counter-productive to the over-all fight against Tirek."

Starlight: "Yes. So, we'll have to find ways to mend fences with as many forces as possible. You did say this is more or less a war of two flags?" She tilted her head.

Duke Condor: "Si. General Falco has risen as the leader of the faction with which we cooperate. He was Commander of the 3rd Division of the Army when this conflict started. He is, however, nominally a Syndicalist.. though he has assured both myself and my cousin that, should we win, a King will be installed as a figurehead with nominal authority to maintain traditions." He frowned. "But, I know him - he craves power. He wants authority. He wishes to be the Supreme Leader of the nation."

K: "Oh, boy, this really reminds me of the Spanish Civil War in my world - though, I admit, there are differences here and there." I shook my head.

Duke Condor: "Would that, perhaps, mean you can be of greater assistance? That, perhaps, you know some of the.. potential.. political pitfalls we might approach?" He seemed very interested, swishing his horns a little.

K: "It does. In my world, the Spanish Civil War was called a dress rehearsal for the largest and most destructive war my world had ever experienced. About 3% of the entire human population, died in that war. Hundreds of millions of people." I nodded slowly. "So, I studied the Spanish Civil War very closely." I pointed at the map. "How much materiel support are the Collectivists receiving from the Herdiet Union, or People's Republic of Khyanga?"

Duke Condor: "Last week, my cousin's soldiers were attcked by an armored box with cannons." He lowered his head a little. "Many Ibex were killed, before one Ibex bravely jumped on it with much lit dynamite."

K: "Great. Someone's giving them tanks." I scowled. "This really IS going to be a Dress Rehearsal for the invasion of Tirek's homelands." And crossed my arms.

Starlight: "We need to get in touch with NEIGHTO - our friends should be arriving at the border, soon. We need more forces, here, and we need armored vehicles.. anti-tank weapons.." She looked at the Duchess. "And, I'm sure, medicine and food for the civilian population, and wounded."

K: "Moreover, I know one step that needs to be taken immediately - Falco HAS to go. Period." I looked at the Duke. "Be honest with me - how popular are you, and your cousin?"

Duke Condor: "Mmm.. Duke Alfonso and I were.. before this, we did not much associate with the other royals, nor the people. I had my lillies, he had his chickens and parrots.. we led simple lives, Mariscal. At Court, we did our best to think of the people.." He sighed deeply. "But, I admit I was out of touch. I am here by accidental events - when the assassination occured, my cousin and I were the ones physically in the same room as the King, and we and our personal attendants helped him escape. As troops defected and volunteers came to the King, my cousin and I helped organize them, lead them, fight.. resist." He lowered his head. "When my brother died.. he did not declare an Heir. Until that moment, it did not matter to my cousin and I who would be the next King, only that Alfonso WAS our king.. and we would die for him. Now.. both of us.. would die for Navarre and her people."

K: "Great. You know, one of the reasons Falco's human analogue managed to win the war and become unquestioned ruler of Spain in my world, was because he built a literal cult of personality around himself. He MADE himself popular." I looked at some of the soldiers outside. "I'm guessing, the majority of your goats follow you out of simple loyalty to the office of Duke."

Duke Condor: "Indeed they do - that and my lucky successes in battle. Of the major factions under Falco, my troops have performed the best and suffered the least. I admit, however, my instruction as a soldier ended with officer training and education at the Royal Military Academy. It is fairly basic instruction.." He trailed off. "..though, only those in the higher classes of society attend, and most of our enemies are not of that social class, so, my cousin and I have been successful in tactics only because our opponents are especially estupido." He chuckled a bit.

K: "Oohh.. I need to bring in my officers. If that's the case, your troops, combined with professional training, and expert officer instruction, could be deadly effective." I stood up. "Since my employer is a royal figure, Starlight, I think we can agree that the Crown of Equestria, at the very least, will support the Crown of Navarre - we will work out who's to be King at a later date." I gestured. "For the time being, I think, for diplomatic reasons, we will address the Crown of Navarre in the name of His Highness, King Alfonso VIII, represented by Duke Condor and Duke Alfonso."

Duke Condor: "..yes, yes, I see that as politically acceptable." He nodded, thoughtfully.

Starlight: "Interesting way to split hairs, K. I like it." She nodded slightly.

K: "Can you get your cousin to agree to a meeting? Soon?" I leaned over a bit, towards the Duke.

Duke Condor: "I think.. with this news, yes. Si! I shall send a messenger at once." He nodded. "Now, to get you back in touch with your people.." He looked towards the door. "..Maria, mi amore, get a guard to find me Lieutenant Espinoza. She can guide them."

Duchess Condor: "Si, mi amore." She got up and scooted off.

An interesting situation. If I let this go, the superior support given by Tirek's forces would no doubt have the lands fall to his control, giving him an invasion route through southern Prance. So, of course, I now have to wade in to a Civil War...

Havoc: C> "Hey, buddeh!"

K: "Rarity, set yourself up." I nodded to her.

Rarity: "Of course, Darling. I get to show off my sniper skills.." She smirked and got into a covered position behind a flat rock.

After a few more minutes, I could hear the crack of Bella's Timberwolf sniper rifle, and I saw a goat's head explode. The defenders were stunned, for a moment, then started looking around for whomever fired the shot. A moment later, Rarity took her own shot from our position, and bagged another goat. Then another precision shot, I guessed Teddy, and another goat fell over, dead. Finally, the defenders got the idea they were under fire, and started to scatter, only for yet another markspony shot to take down an Officer.

I could see Havoc's wolves moving to the western edge of the town, and infiltrate the streets like shadows. Within about two minutes, there was shooting from both the east and west ends of town, then the sounds of wolves attacking in close quarters, and savagely ripping goats apart.

Lt. Espinoza: "My Word!" She recoiled a bit. "You have.. Canids?!"

K: "One of my best friends." I smirked. "Don't worry, stay next to me, they won't eat you. Let's go, everyone!" I ushured my small group forwards.

We charged forwards, weapons raised. I kept mine pointed forwards, looking for targets, but the enemy soldiers had all scattered like flies. I finally sighted one in, that was trying to direct troops, and put three rounds into its chest, sending it to the ground. I pivoted, and fired an HE shell from the Gavin into a sandbag pile, and blew it apart, sending three terrified goats running from it, and right into Havoc's team. All three were grabbed - from the left, then the right, then tackled straight down forwards - and eviscerated with wolf teeth.

I moved to the center of town, with Espinoza and Firecracker. Firecracker pointed to a cluster of enemy troops packed behind some crates and overturned carts.

K: "I'll drop an egg on 'em." I opened the Gavin and loaded a shell.

Firecracker: "No need, sir."

She focused her magic into her horn, it glowed RED with heat, and she summoned up a rack of magical bottle rockets, which she fired off at their position, one after another, like an MLRS. The impacts were Grenade-strength, and after firing twelve, there wasn't much left but small craters and body parts.

Sacquet: "EAST SIDE CLEAR!" He called out, from the edge of the Plaza.

Havoc: "WEST SIDE CLEAR!" He returned the call from the other side.

Norfy: "NORTH CLEAR!" He called out, approaching from the north side.

K: "South clear." I stated. "Bring it in, lads and lasses. Sweep the rest of the town for hold-outs. Then, we're having a meeting in that building there." I pointed to the Police Building we'd been detained at.

Now, the real challenge - wading in to a Civil War, picking a side, and helping them win not only the combat phase, but the peace phase.. then turning them around and getting them to help us fight Tirek. It did occur to me that, now, the Ibexians would be my most combat-experienced troops, after my own..

Part 3: Iberian Jam Session

TIME: 14:11 IT
DATE: DATE: War Day 732/6 Quartus, 1004 C.S.
LOCATION: South of Girul, Navarre

Almost everyone had come. Celestia was even here, though she'd stayed under cover when the shooting started. Forest Spirit and Blinda were at the planning table I'd set up.

He was tan-coated, pegging him as a Southern Spanish Ibex. His uniform was slate grey, and he wore an oversized Gorro de Cuartel Cap with longer tassles, in addition to a cloak with a white underface.

I noted, also, that the main difference between them was that Alfonso's rank and ribbons were in blue, while Condor's were red. Both wore otherwise-identical uniform styles.

Duke Alfonso: "Ahh.. bon dia per a voste, Mariscal.." He then saw Celestia, and both he and Condor bowed to her. "La seva majestat! S¢c el Duc Alfons." He took her hoof, and nodded to it.

I noted Duke Alfons spoke Catalan, not Spanish/Navarese.

Duke Condor: "Your Majesty, you grace us with your presence. Duke Condor, m'lday." He took his turn accepting her hoof.

K: "These are the two I want to back. Ibexeria is in the throes of a Civil War, Your Highness." I shook my head. "Tirek is involved - we either pick a side and fight this war, or let him have an invasion route into southern Europe."

Celestia: "My GOODness..!" She was shocked at this. "I.. I had no idea." She turned to the Dukes. "I am sorry, I had no idea.."

K: "And it's kinda my fault this ended up being so bloody - they were the other customers of that espionage group we caught. They've copied my weapons designs and made their own." I nodded.

Celestia: "So, you want to have NEIGHTO - more specifically, Equestria - sponsor the royalists?" She looked at the Dukes.

K: "Yes, Cel. I do. They're the best bet we have of a strong government in the post-war era." I raised my head a bit, then looked at Duke Alfonso. "Duke Alfonso, my condolences on the loss of your family."

Duke Alfonso: "I thank you, Mariscal K. The King was the best of us, and his son, was our bright future flame." He shook his head. "I will say right now, Duke Condor and I have been talking - we both agree to jointly represent the Royal Government of Navarre as equals for the time being, in the name of the King." He nodded. "Living or dead, until another is crowned. In light of the possibility of receiving such aide, it is our duty as Royals to put the best interest of our Goats first, and our personal issues, last." He looked to Celestia and nodded to her. "You represent a real chance at victory. This.. this cannot be ignored."

Fluttershy: "Well. I'm glad the two of you can at least be friends.." She smiled warmly to them both.

Duke Condor: "Ah. Before the war, we were as friendly as one might expect - we saw each other in Court four times a year, perhaps went to family events together. Social, but not close." He shrugged a bit.

Duke Alfonso: "Indeed. Another failing of ours, Cousin." He put his hoof on Condor's shoulder. "I feel as though our inattentiveness only helped the enemies we didn't know we had."

Duke Condor: "On that, I can fully agree, Cousin." He put his own hoof on Alfonso's shoulder.

K: "Well, THIS is a good start." I nodded. "I was hoping you two would agree to this - now, perhaps, we can unite the other Nationalist groups under one fully unified banner, instead of multiple teams in general cooperation." I shook my head a bit.

Duke Condor: "We are Royals - it is our duty to put The People first. If we don't, we do not deserve their loyalty." He nodded.

K: "Now, the big issue." I nodded to Duke Alfonso. "Did Duke Condor explain that I'm from another world?"

Duke Alfonso: "He did.. but.." He gesutured. "..while I understand that humans must be violent creatures moreso than us - no offense, senyor -"

K: "None taken." I nodded.

Duke Alfonso: "- I don't quite understand what this means, beyond that you have the knowledge to produce firearms and conduct grand strategy." He gestured a bit, with one hoof.

K: "Put simply, this war you're engaging in, has a very, very close analogue in the human world. Moreover, many events in this world, match events in mine. Like a song that rhymes with another. It allows me to navigate perils that I know for certain will come up, by guiding events to a much better outcome. Siding with you two, for example? In my world, a man named Francisco Franco siezed control of the nation that mirrors Navarre, and continuously kept his rivals - you, for example - off balance and impotent. While Spain was a nice place, generally, during his rule, he built up a cult of personality that ensured Spain would begin to crumble again after his death. Which, in many ways, it did." I shrugged. "Your own General Fernando Falco? From what I understand, he's already started on that path. If he is permitted to crown himself supreme ruler of Navarre, yes, your people might have a few prosperous decades.. BUT.. afterwards?" I opened my hands and gently swung them out. "I doubt that he'd permit Navarre to have anything to do with any of the Pony kingdoms, and in our struggle against Tirek, provide only token support. The Pony kingdoms - not to mention the dragons - WILL remember this, and when Falco is gone, they won't forget that the Ibexians paid them mere lip service in exchange for blood and treasure. Which will lead to hard feelings, isolation on a growingly global community, and eventually, economic stagnation, as trade will virtually grind to a halt." I looked to both of them. "My counterpart to Navarre eventually avoided this by prostrating and degrading itself on the world stage with intentionally weak politics, dirty financial deals, and finally, humiliation as cowards, followed by a virtual Reconquista of their lands by foreign invaders, displacing the locals." I shook my head.

Duke Alfonso: "..my word." He suddenly became very serious. "If what you say is true in any measure.. what do we do?" He lifted his head to look at me. "General Falco is already seen as our most effective leader.. many soldiers respect him." He shrugged. "And he has agreed privately to restore the Throne, but as a mere figurehead.. the people would likely accept this." He looked between himself and Duke Condor. "Neither my cousin nor I have that kind of popular support. If the fighting were to stop now, with us as victors, no matter which side you supported for the Throne? Would not be accepted by many."
