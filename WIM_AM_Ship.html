Kingdom of Ambleion: Warships (Royal Ambleonian Navy)



The Royal Navy of Ambleion has a tradition stretching back hundreds of years. Given the relative peace of Equus, most of this tradition is just seakeeping, sailing, occasional rescues of ponies lost at sea, and some minor conflict with pirates, but never anything serious. At the time of Tirek's assault in 1002 C.S., not a sailor alive had seen anything approaching "serious" combat. However, the small Ambleonian fleet was renown for their prowess on the seas, and boasted an impressive record. The transition to a combat navy was not smooth, however, even though they had talented shipwrights, and regularly used steel hull vessels already. As with most Equian navies, they lacked officers with command experience, and it showed.
The Crystal Kingdom used the prefix "HMAS" for "Her Majesty's Ambleonian Ship" for each of their larger vessels with proper names. By tradition, Pendant/Pennant Numbers in the RAN do not include a letter prefix for Capital Ships - this was usually for larger sailing vessels, and by 989 C.S., for the steel hull vessels Prince of Wales-class patrol ships.

The massive Inflexible-class battleships are the largest vessels ever made in Ambleion. The second largest were the Queen of Thames Cruise Ships, at 1012 meters. The first ships designed with weapons, the construction went smoothly, as the designers used the basic drawing plans of the Celestia-class Battleships, simpyl altering measurements where needed. The ships are fairly stout, and are armored enough to take hits from guns up to 13-inch. The Inflexible-class are right on the line of what would be considered a "Dreadnaught" owing to their 6-inch secondary battery.

The County-class cruisers are an up-scale of the Prince of Wales-class patrol ships, which were nearing the end of their service lives when the Tirek situation arose. The Prince of Wales-class had no armament, other than Ballista of various sizes. Redesigning the general shape of the Prince of Wales-class to accomodate gun turrets, the designers came up with a solid, well constructed Armored Cruiser, proof to guns up to 9-inch across "the vitals" (engine spaces, lower waterline compartments).

The Town-class scout cruisers are the agile eyes of the Ambleonian fleet. Packing enough firepower to tackle enemies above thier weight class, or simply to lay down a torpedo spread to permit escape, the Town-class are considerably lighter than the Inflexible-class and County-class, with light armor only proffed to guns of 5-inch and below across most of the ship's reinforced sections, though 6-inch over the vitals.

The "heavy backup" to the Bird-class corvettes, intended to drive off interceptors, the Family-class Frigates are low-slung, long-ish vessels with a high caliber main gun for their ship type, giving them impressive performance against enemy Destroyers, Frigates, and Corvettes. Not QUITE as fast as the Bird-class corvettes, these Frigates can still, in a relatively close range engagement, stay close enough behind at full steam to provide full covererage with thier main guns.

These small vessels are the fast attackers of the Ambleonian fleet battle line. Armed with three torpedo launchers, and the highest speed of any Amleonian ship, they can dart in, lay smoke, fire torpedoes, and withdraw very rapidly. The 530mm Torpedo Launchers each have one full reload, letting lightning strike twice. These were based on steel hulled "racing schooners" that were popular around the Dover area from 980 C.S. until the war.
