Chapter 1: Redemption of an Old Man

Part 1: One Again Returned

TIME: 17:32 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 1/7 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Forest near Princesston

K: "So. We better camp here for the night." I shrugged.

Sunny: "Yeah." She nodded. "Uhm.. I have oats!" She smiled.

K: "Mm..." I smiled a bit. "..I kinda miss Ration Oats." I shrugged. "So plain, but so filling." I nodded to her. "It's like a hug in the stomach from Celestia herself, letting you know, it'll all be okay." I stared into the fire as she started getting water on to boil. "I can't recall how many times I came upon soldiers just having.. sharing.. oats and whatever they could toss in there.." I blinked, letting the memories come. "..Rapid Bearing. He was from Ariezona. Good stallion." I nodded. "He liked a little cream in his." I shrugged a tiny bit. "Dusty Dunes." I smiled. "I loved her. She.. she liked anything in there. Turkey Jerky was a favorite." I looked to the side. "My home is beyond the dead zone."

Sunny: "Yeah." She smiled a bit, and nuzzled up under my left arm. "But, I'm here, General. You've got me."

K: "Just K." I nodded. "Just like the old days. I'm not General or Sir, I'm just K."

Sunny: "You got it, K!" She nodded firmly.. then looked at the buttons on her satchel, gently tracing them with her hoof. "What were they like? The Elements?"

K: "Fluttershy. Friend to all animals. Wife of Golden Wattle. She'd been.." I shrugged a bit. "..the war hurt her in ways I never thought was possible to recover from, but she did." I nodded. "Rarity Seeker. Wife of Dusk Seeker, who ended the war as Commander of Luna's Guard. She designed our uniforms.." I nodded to her collar. "..and those rank pins." I beamed a grin. "Pinkie Pie. First Element of Laughter. Always ready for..." I trailed off. "...I have to see if I can find The Magic Mirror... if it's not broken.. I might be able to get her back. We need her." I nodded. "Rainbow Dash-Buran. Wife of Gilda Buran. Daughter-in-law to the Prime Minister of the Griffon Kingdom. Fastest pony alive. Always there to cheer you on and make your day brighter." I smiled. "Twilight Sentry - nee Sparkle. First Element of Magic. So consumed by science and its ideals that she often forgot the Equestrian element. Until the war opened her eyes, and her heart. Her husband, Flash, was the Commander of the Solar Guard."

Sunny: "They.. all sound so amazing." She sat in awe, and took the oats off the fire.

K: "The.." I screwed my eyes shut. "..one I miss most of all.." I swallowed hard. "Applejack. My.. my wife.." I let the tears come. "She's gone, too." I just stopped.. and sat in silence for a bit as I was handed oats in a bowl. "I lost my human wife, too. And the rest of my family." I looked at Sunny. "Now, more than ever, I am ready to die to save all of you." I shook my head. "I wish Broken Dawn was here. He.. he knew about all this." I gave a dismissive wave and ate.

Sunny: "No matter what!" She nodded firmly. "You have me. As I said. K." She smiled a bit. "The future needs you. We need you." She tilted her head off to the side a bit. "Maybe I do, too. You're the answer to so much."

K: "Answer. And I'm the one with the questions." I shook my head. "Who is there in the government?" I nodded to her.

Sunny: "Well, we have a partial Parliament - the Parliament in Exile. They use the old State Capital building in Providence, Rowed Island. Then there's Prime Minister Haven." She shook her head. "She's.. she goes between the towns with her daughters, and tries to do what she can."

K: "Who are her daughters?" I shrugged.

Sunny: "Well, her one daughter, Pipp, is really famous." She pulled out her cell phone again, and showed me a video by her. It reminded me of a YouTuber "Idol" Format - said Talent does a thing in each episode, and the episode is opened and closed in a specific, ritualistic way. "I've never met them in person."

K: "We will." I nodded.

Sunny: "I'm just so fascinated by you." She smiled a bit. "I mean, there's stories about you and the War against Tirek, but.. some people were thinking you weren't quite real. Like, you were some kind of narrative allegory for a collection of other ponies, or something." She gestured a bit.

K: "No, I'm real, and I'm me." I shrugged a bit, smiling at her. "I'd almost forgotten how cute you ponies are."

Sunny: "OH!" She blushed. "K! Really. W-We've just met."

K: "Have we? It sounds like you know me pretty well already." I nodded to her. "I'm the one at a disadvantage, here."

Sunny: "..well, yes.." She blushed harder. "..I've thought about this moment for years." She looked down, embarrassed. "But, I keep wondering, do I really deserve it?"

K: "I think you do." I nodded, smiling.

Sunny got up and came over to me, and slid into my lap. A big part of her was playing out some fantasy she'd had for many years, something that probably occupied her thoughts for a very long time, and something she'd imagined over and over again. And I just gave her clearance to go ahead with it.

Sunny: "K.. I.." She looked up into my eyes, and I could see a sparkle there..

K: "..maybe later, I'll ah.. get you something nice. Take you out to eat.." I let my fingers run through her mane.

I had to admit, it'd been a long time since I felt like this.

Sunny: "Mmmm.." She grinned, and her eyes briefly crossed as I skritched her ears just right. "..love some apple pie.."

K: "Oh?" I smirked. "Anything else?" I said, in low, soft tones.

Sunny: "Mmmmmm... candy apple.. apple fritters.. apple dumplings.."

I froze.

K: "What.. ah.. what's your full name, Sunny?" I cocked a brow.

Sunny: "Sunrise Magic Starscout." She smiled, rubbing into my hand. "My dad said.. our line goes back over ten generations.."

K: "Ah." I blinked. "Sunrise Magic.. is.. a breed.. of apple." I blinked.

OH SHIT.

OHHHHHH SHIIIIIIIIT.

FUCKFUCFUCKUFKCUFJCKJFKJSDKLF...

I shot up out of her grasp and her off my lap and zipped up as fast as I could. I was so un-erect, I swear the boys retreated inside!

Sunny: "W-What's the matter?!" She was shocked, having been rolled on her flank.

K: "YOU'RE MY GRAND-DAUGHTER!" I pointed at her. "I don't CARE if it's been TEN generations - you're MY KID!" I shook my head rapidly. "Ooohhh... oh, no, that's incest!" I pointed at her.

Sunny: "INCEST?!" She looked very hurt and offended at first, then sat down, and started thinking.. hard.. tapping the side of her jaw, and then finally shaking her head. "Wow.. I.. I guess it is, huh!?" She looked at her hooves. "Wow. I.. I almost made a big mistake." She looked very sad, about then. "That.. I'm sorry."

I sat next to her and put my arm over her shoulder.

K: "Look, Sunny. You.. your mom and dad have passed, right? Well, instead of your lover, if.. you wanna call me Grandpa.." I one-arm hugged her. "..I couldn't be prouder to have a grand daughter like you."

Sunny: "OH!" She launched herself into my arms, and hugged me. "I'll TAKE it! I'll TAKE it! Family!? I'll take it." And cried a little. "Family.. grandpa."

K: "Grand-daughter." I hugged her back.

So, I had arrived in the world, met a pony, and.. well, almost made a mistake. But, now, I had a.. I had family. No matter how bad this future was, I had family, now, and that was enough. It would always be enough.

[End of Part 1]

Part 2: New Friend

TIME: 06:52 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 2/8 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Forest near Princesston

Izzy: "HI, NEW FRIEND!" She popped into my view as I slowly woke up.

K: "Hi, new friend." I just smiled, and reached up, and rubbed her neck. "I'm K. Who're you?"

Sunny: "Mmmh? What?" She popped an eye open, and realized there was a unicorn about ten inches from her snout, given that she was laying close to me. "AAAH! Whoa!" She slid out of her covers, and skidded back.

Izzy: "I'm IZZY!" She giggled. "Izzy Moonbow!"

K: "I'm K. But.. you knew that already, didn't you?" I smiled, and slid up to a seated position. "Sunny, why don't you make our guest some tea? And breakfast." I nodded to her.

Sunny: "Ah-we-uh-sure." She stammered a bit, before the calmness in my voice soothed her. "So, you're a Unicorn! I've never really gotten to talk to a Unicorn before. I've got so many questions.. why are you here?"

Izzy: "This!" She held out a faded piece of paper, with drawings on it, as Sunny started to get the fire built up from embers, again.

Sunny: "Ah, I'm Sunny Starscout.. oh!" She took the paper, and looked at it. "Izzy.. I DREW THIS!" She smiled. "When I was just a Filly!"

Izzy: "Yeah?!" She hopped around a bit. "Then that means we've been friends all our lives!!"

K: "Good way to look at it." I nodded, and got my boots on. "So, what.. was that paper the whole reason you came out here?"

Izzy: "KIiiiiinda?" She tilted her head. "I've never been out of Bridlewood before." She blinked and looked down. "Not a lot of ponies leave. Some do, but.." She shrugged a bit sadly. "..lots of them feel like just Unicorns should live there.."

K: "Don't be sad." I leaned over and hugged her neck - she smelled of flowers, glue, and a soldering iron all at once. "You know who I am, right?"

Izzy: "YEAH!" She leaned into the hug. "The stories were right! You hug good!" She threw her legs over me, and gave me a full body hug. "But.. that means.. if you're here..." She pulled back.

K: "I'm going to do everything I can to fix everything here." I nodded. "I promise."

Izzy: "It's.." She blinked, tilted her head at me, nodded like I'd understand what she was about to say, and continued. "..it's Jinxies to talk about you too much, except on certain days. Like, most unicorns think talking about you too much or at the wrong times could jinx us - that you won't come back, or you come back and it's bad, bad."

K: "Well, I'm here. For better or worse." I groaned as I stretched - I'd slept in a bad position.

Izzy: "Aww.. aren't you supposed to be.. like.. a super Ponidonis or something?" She poked her nose at my ancient middle aged body.

K: "I'm 44, Izzy. Humans peak at about 35. I'm past my prime." I shrugged. "Not saying I can't get back into the shape I was, but it'll take time and effort." I nodded. "Just like making friends." I pat her snout. "That's a cool charm bracelet." I nodded to it. "Not many ponies back in the past wore stuff like that."

Izzy: "OH!" She held it up and waved it in my face a bit. "I have.. like.. I like to make these for friends." She reached into her own saddle bag, leaned over and put one on Sunny. "Here.. I made this for you." She smiled at her as Sunny made tea.

Sunny: "I have.. so many questions." She smiled. "None of the other unicorns I've ever met ever want to talk about themselves."

K: "And that right there is part of the problem." I nodded. "Do you two know the story of the Winter of Hard Feelings?" I looked at them.

They both shook their heads 'no' - Izzy with more energy, Sunny with more reverence.

K: "Yeah, I need to.. tell you your own history." I nodded, and shrugged. "Ironic - a human that has to teach ponies about pony history." I gestured as Sunny handed me a canteen cup with Red Tea.

Izzy: "Oooo! Red Fireleaf Tea?!" She accepted the cup gingerly. "I usually like a smoothie in the morning."

K: "Oh, really?" I smirked.

Sunny: "I loooove making smoothies!" She cheered up. "I have a smoothie machine back home." She tilted her head. "I wonder.."

K: "You should have a side gig. I can't imagine being a conspiracy theorist and a Militia officer pays all the bills with extra." I nodded.

Sunny: "Well, I do okay.. but.. yeah." She smiled. "Great idea, grandpa!"

Izzy: "GRANDPAAAAAA?" She got excited again.

K: "She's descended from my daughter Liberty Apple." I nodded, smiling, as we sipped the tea, waiting for the ration oats to soak.

Izzy: "OH MY HOOFNESS!" She bounced again. "Oh, wow, Sunny! That's sooooo amazing!"

Sunny: "Yeah.." She said, wistfully. "..I wish you'd met my dad, Argyle. He was even more obsessed with you than I was."

K: "Where ever he is, looking down on us now, I'm positive he's happy and knows that you'll be well looked after - both as family, and as a General." I patted her neck.

Sunny: "I guess he is!" She smiled.

Izzy: "So.. what are YOU TWO doing out here?" She piped up.

K: "I just got here last night. Sunny says that Bridlewood is closer to here than Maretime Bay, and I should head there to meet with people." I shrugged.

Izzy: "OH! Yeah! It's about a day's trot from here. Maybe a bit more? I dunno - everyone says I trot fast!" She giggled.

K: "I can see why." I smiled a bit. "You have a lot of energy." And shrugged. "Given my condition, I'll probably slow things down to another over night out here in the woods."

Izzy: "Oooo... that's dangerous." She blinked. "There's lots of nasty things out here!"

K: "We're well armed." I nodded. "You carrying?"

Izzy: "Yup!" She pulled out a Roc and a large knife. "Gotta stay safe out here."

K: "We'll be okay, then." I nodded, reassuringly.

Izzy: "..ah.." She grew calm. "..not against the Darklies." And looked away.

K: "Explain." I remained calm, myself.

Izzy: "The shadow creature things made by The Deep Darkness. They can eat us. Worse.. uhm.. bullets don't hurt them much." She shrugged a bit.

K: "I see." I nodded. "And as Sunny explained, you've all gone a thousand years with no magic."

Izzy: "..yah." She shrugged a bit, then went over to a tree. "BUT! I can do THIS!" She essentially started jackhammering her horn into the tree, and cut through it no problem.

When she came back over, I made a point to gesture to her to come closer so I could touch it. It felt.. more.. solid. Like a crystal.

K: "Do you feel that, at all?" I said, stroking it gently.

Izzy: "Nah. I mean, like, I know you're touching it, but I don't, like feel anything from it." She blinked. "Should I?"

K: "Evolution works fast, here." I nodded a bit. "Unicorn horns used to be VERY sensitive. I don't even think this is keratin any more. It feels like crystal." I looked at them both. "Keratin is basically what hoofy nails are made from. Unicorn horns used to be made of that, with a very thin membrane under the outer surface that was VERY sensitive to touch." I looked at Izzy. "Do unicorns still 'kiss' by rubbing horns together?"

Izzy turned BRIGHT RED and slunk back a bit.

K: "Yeah, that's.. not a normal kiss any longer." I shrugged. "My apologies. I'm working with thousand-year-old cultural standards."

Izzy: "I thought so, so.. I didn't say anything." She smiled a bit, though.

Sunny: "I know you just came from there, and everything, but we need to get to Bridlewood." She shrugged. "K needs to meet everypony there, and.." She looked at me. "..ponies need to know he's back."

Izzy: "Okay!" She bounced a bit. "No problem. I know the best way there." She smiled. "Howeverrrrrrr.." She rubbed her fetlock on her chin a moment. "..there might be an easier way, if we take the OTHER way..."

Sunny: "Other way?" She tilted her head.

Izzy: "Yeah." She nodded excitedly. "Once we're done with breakfast.." She started eating quickly.

Sunny: "I usually make pancakes at home.." She smiled a bit.

K: "Been a minute since I had a good pancake." I nodded. "I look forwards to it."

Izzy: "Ooooo.. real earth pony pancakes." She thought about it in amazement.

K: "One thing at a time." I nodded, grinning. "You're so full of energy." I pet her mane again, and she leaned into it. "Oh, you like mane pets, huh?"

Izzy: "Feels good.." She said, smiling, relaxed a bit. "..I knew it would, too."

Sunny: "My Grandpa." She slid over and got up under my other hand.

K: "Good thing I have two of these." I smiled and scratched them both.

After finishing breakfast, we gathered everything up, and started heading towards Bridlewood. It was a pretty peaceful trip, and Sunny and Izzy spent the time talking to each other about what Terran or Earth Ponies did, versus Unicorns. I absorbed this information, and found that Unicorns had fallen heavily into superstition over the last thousand years. I didn't quite catch everything she was saying, as I was huffing and puffing like a chain smoker after less than a mile, though I kept up with them as best I could.

I was, indeed, slowing them down. I knew it. But, it was inevitable. I was old, I was out of shape, and I'd need to get back into shape if I was going to be able to do this again. I knew I needed my old stuff back - my magical items. All of them.

My pace was slowed by age. Instead of being able to push myself to a 25+ mile day, averaging 10 hours of marching at an average speed of 2.5 miles an hour, I was barely getting 1.5 miles an hour. The girls were strolling along, and I was solidly marching.

Sunny: "You okay, Grandpa?" She dropped back.

K: "Just not used to this, that's all." I shrugged. "Been a while since I did this."

Izzy: "It'll be okay." She slid back as well.

K: "Sorry I didn't come as a super man, out of the box, girls." I shrugged.

Sunny: "Oh, Grandpa!" She giggled.


TIME: 09:37 NERST
LOCATION: Ashokan Lake, Erie, New Equestria


K: "Hey, Ashokan Lake.." I smiled.

I walked to the edge of the lake, and I was about to stick my hand in the water, when I pulled back, just ahead of Izzy trying to grab my arm with her mouth.

K: "..bad idea?" I asked, looking at her.

Izzy: "Bad idea." She nodded.

Sunny: "What was it like a thousand years ago?" She smiled, tilting her head at me.

K: "Not dangerous." I shrugged. "What's in there?" I asked Izzy.

Izzy: "Dark things." She shrugged, saying that dismissively. "But, we can get across the lake on a boat." She nodded firmly.

K: "DO we have a boat?" I tilted my head at them.

Sunny: "Not pre-made, but if we use my shelter half and some other materials, I think we can make a canoe." She shrugged.

Izzy: "OH! I know EXACTLY what to do!" She tugged at Sunny. "Empty out your pack! Leave this to me!" She tapped her hooves on the ground, excitedly.

I did likewise, and let her take the lead. I knew these ponies well enough to know that, when one declares themself an expert, you best listen. I was reminded immediately of Rapid Bearing.. and I missed him badly.

Over the next hour, Izzy patched together a workable boat from the items in our packs, and some lumber she harvested from the surrounding forest. As she did this, I talked to Sunny at length for more details of what I missed. She didn't have a lot of solid answers, as it WAS a thousand years ago - and she pointed out something that was pointed out to me in the Great War: How much do I know about what happened a thousand years before me?

Truth is, I didn't know much about 1026AD. So, it stands to reason that only a very few stories from 1026 C.S. would survive until today, right? Right. The general information that the War happened, and "General K" was the hero, and that he MIGHT have been a human, were the main take-aways from that era. That and, a cataclysm happened, Celestia, Luna, Sunset, Cadence, and Twilight vanished. Magic was lost. Theia knows the details, but she doesn't explain them, because she doesn't want ponies to worry about them endlessly, when they can't do anything about them.

Izzy was nothing short of a miracle worker. With the things in our packs, and some lumber, she had a very solid raft made. And three paddles. The raft even had a small sail and mast, and a rudder.

What got me curious is that she decorated it with some glitter that she had.

K: "Hmmmm." I examined the glitter-touched oars. "..keeps the dark things away, does it?"

Izzy: "Oh, yeah!" She said, smiling. "YOU get it!" She pranced a tiny bit. "I just knew you'd understand. Yeah, glitter adds that extra sparkle to things and it helps keep the dark things.. it wards them off." She seemed very upset that they even existed.

K: "Well, you did a fine job." I nodded, and got on, with Sunny. "I can only imagine how inventive you can be." And smiled.

Izzy: "Awww.." She blushed a bit. "It's just a bit of Unicycling!"

Sunny: "What's.. Unicycling?" She looked over at her new friend.

Izzy: "Taking something old - lots of somethings old sometimes - and making something NEW!" She beamed a grin ten miles wide.

K: "She's MacGuyver." I chuckled, pushing off the shore with my oar.

They both looked at me, confused.

Both: "Who's MacGuyver?"

Oh, boy.. I forgot. I'll have to explain television shows all over again, but, at least now, they'd understand the concept.

[End of Part 2]

Part 3: Sailing Away

TIME: 11:03 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 2/8 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Ashokan Lake, Erie, New Equestria


We were making excellent time. The improvised sail was working very well to pull us along. The raft was wide enough that even with a rather brisk wind, we didn't feel unsteady at all. The girls were chatting with each other - Sunny had 142 questions for Izzy, and expressed great remorse that no other Unicorns she'd seen before, wanted to chat about them.

Izzy: "..I mean, I can open a tin can with my horn, and all, but that's kinda messy.." She blushed a bit. "I never thought I'd end up having SUCH a good FRIEND!" She slid over to me. "Here, K." And she put a friendship bracelet on my wrist.

It had a little sun, moon, and heart carved into these clear stones that I could not identify. As I rotated it, I saw an apple, a diamond, a butterfly, a lightning bolt, a paint brush, a balloon, and a star-with-sun as well. I choked up immediately.. and had to sniffle hard.

Izzy: "..are you okay?" She put her hoof on my shoulder.

K: "I have no idea how you knew, but.." I held up my right wrist. "..now I know they'll always watch over me."

Izzy: "You seem like you have soooo many things about you, that just ONE Mark isn't enough!" She smiled.

K: "These were my friends." I nodded to the bracelet. "I have... their memories.. I have to honor. I have to fix whatever went wrong, somehow. You all deserve to live in peace." I shrugged, but my emotions were getting to me.

A thousand years. And I had no idea what exactly had happened. I needed to know. I needed to know the full truth of what was going on, why, and how. And moreover, I needed to know what it was that I was here to stop, prevent, change, or rescue.

We made excellent time the 12+ miles up stream. When we transitioned from lake to stream, the wind was still with us most of the way, but we ended up using oars for the last half of it, moving us over shallower parts of the stream by force. It was still much faster than walking. We arrived at the former site of the town of Shandaken in just a few hours.

TIME: 14:47 NERST
LOCATION: Ruins of Shandaken, Erie, New Equestria

We disembarked from our raft, but made sure to pull it as far on shore as we could, so we could possibly use it later. We didn't know how long the trip to Bridlewood might take us, so, we took the sail (Sunny's shelter half) as well.

Izzy: "She was a good lil' raft, wasn't she?" She beamed a grin.

K: "We got two mares up a stream, so, I think so, Izzy." I shrugged. "I know the route to Bridlewood from here. It's almost a straight shot north, about 12 miles." I nodded. "I think I can make it the whole way." A gesture, dismissive, and a shrug. "Granted, it'll take eight hours. So, we have a choice, ladies. Camp out in the town ruins for the night, and take the extra daylight we have left, to forage, or we march hard and hope to get to Bridlewood about midnight."

Sunny: "I bet when you were in good shape, you could've done that march a lot faster." She nodded.

K: "I got to the point where I was doing about three and a half miles an hour." I paused. "You know, I noticed you didn't correct me on Miles to Country Miles to Kilometers."

Sunny: "Well.. we kinda use both the old system and the new one." She shrugged. "Military tends to use kilometers and meters and centimeters and such, but a lot of civilians started using the country mile to measure distance, and just shortened it back down to mile." She shrugged with one hoof upturned.

K: "Goodness me." I chuckled.

Izzy: "Hoofness." She corrected, gently.

K: "Hoofness." I smirked. "For you. I haven't any hooves. But, I do have these." I leaned over and gave her ear skritchies.. she hummed pleasantly.

Sunny: "I think we should rest, Grandpa." She nodded. "You're right, you're not in shape any more. And the last thing we need is you out of condition in the woods with a monster coming around." She looked at Izzy. "I don't think we could protect you by ourselves, with two pistols."

K: "Good point. I presume a nice big fire will keep the nasties away..?" I led on, as we marched into the town ruins.

Sunny: "You're right, Grandpa." She smiled. "I'm.. I'm still getting used to that. Grandpa." She thumped her head to my hip. "My Grandpa."

K: "You'll never be alone again, Sunny." I nodded. "I promise. At least not until my job here is finished, whatever it is."

We got to the center of town, and I realized that while it was indeed abandoned, it had not been abandoned for more than perhaps a hundred years. There were signs of struggle, too - some bullet holes in a few walls. The town courthouse was surprisingly in very good condition, so we decided to shelter there. The large glass dome over the entrance hall was long shattered by weather, and the natural depression in the middle that led to a now-dead tree made a perfect camp site.

K: "I have a good knife for batonning wood, but I'd prefer an axe." I shrugged. "I guess you didn't plan on large fires, did you?"

Sunny: "Just a cooking fire each night - easy enough to gather up enough wood for that." She shrugged.

Izzy: "An axe.." She paced off.

K: "Something's up." I nodded. "Come on, let's gather what we can and get the fire started. I have a feeling we'll have an axe to clear the large logs, soon."

Sunny: "Well, I can gather the wood." She shrugged. "You know the good plants to eat, right? That really hasn't changed. Why don't you forage, Grandpa?" She smiled. "You need to get back in practice, too."

K: "Natural leader, ain'cha." I smirked. "I'd say that's my genes, but I know who's those are - Forest Spirit." I nodded. "Alright, I'll be back."

So, I went off into the town. I had never been to this town during the war, but I had heard about it from somewhere. I wandered a bit, keeping my AR-15 in low ready. I had 450 rounds with me, and my current tactical rig held six magazines. That should be plenty. As I checked a few buildings, I re familiarized myself with transitioning to my pistol. The old ways.. I had to remember, I had to get this back down pat.

I needed my soldier's instincts sharpened again.

After about half an hour, I found a town community garden, long over grown. But, it bore fruit - literally. Equus-native fruits that I'd long missed. Large, fat Blueberries were mixed in with Colmaberries, an apple-sized berry that tasted like a blackberry crossed with a Granny Smith apple.

Granny Smith.. did you have to live to see the end? I missed her immediately, thinking about her. I put my hand to the ground.

K: "Granny, if you can hear me wherever you are, I love you still. Grandson to Grandma. I'll do everything I can for our family, I promise." I patted the soil, then went about picking the fruits.

After I gathered those, I went and grabbed some huge potatoes, and carrots. As for meat.. well.. I'd have to get inventive. Fluttershy wasn't around to offend, so, I kept watch for deer or hog. What I spotted was a Quozat - a hog-like member of the rodent family, native to Equus. They had a reputation for waiting garbage, but in the wild, they feasted on tubers and roots, along with a side diet of insects. One round from my AR-15 put it down with ease.

I trucked all this back to City Hall, and found Izzy had, indeed, constructed a scrap axe from debris, and was cutting the logs. The axe head looked like a ground down fire axe head, and the haft was a long stick, somewhat hastily formed into an axe handle.
