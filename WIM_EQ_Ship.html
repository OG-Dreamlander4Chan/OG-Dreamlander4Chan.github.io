Kingdom of Equestria: Warships (Royal Equestrian Navy)



Equestria, initially, did not have an armed Navy. The Royal Guard maintained a small fleet of small sailing vessels for search and rescue operations, and the Royal Yacht, a Schooner-sized vessel, for pleasure excursions. Constructing a fighting navy from the ground up, General K had to initially adapt to the shipbuilding capability of the kingdom as it existed. After the 2nd Attack on Canterlot, however, the Steel Fleet Program was pushed forwards, despite the technical limitations of existing shipbuilding industry. While steel ships could be made from the very beginning, they had no concept of how ship armor worked at the time, and could not have constructed a warship. Hence, an initial reduction in technological era to facilitate faster shipbuilding and allow for the warships themselves to be self-repairable in the field, instead of outright armored to withstand attacks.
Equestria has several major naval facilities, but the Navy itself is headquarted at Fillydephia Naval Yards, in Docksylvania. Other facilities include Baleston Naval Yards, Manehattan Naval Shipyards, and New Haven Naval Base. The Port of Mayaimi also has a dedicated set of docks for the Equestrian Navy that later became Saletine Naval Base.
Equestria uses the prefix "HMS" (Her Majesty's Ship) for its vessels. As Equestria is afforded the leading place in North Equus, all other nations with Royal governments use a prefix variant denoting their nation.

Equestria's first warships, the Bellerophon-class screw frigates did not serve for a very long time, as they were quickly superceded by the Steel Fleet Program. Parts of the original Bellerophons were used to create the follow-on steel Bellerophon-class Frigates, including the base frame and uprights, though all parts were replaced with steel.

The crown jewel of the Steel Fleet, the Celestia-class battleships are based on no one specific ship design, but are generally designed after steel hull warships of the United States Navy circa 1880 - 1900. The color scheme is even similar to "The Great White Fleet" by default (though, for combat operations, a grey paintjob is applied). These large vessels have substantial striking power, but as Equestria "went first" in constructing a combat navy, most of her peers exceeded their designs by some measure, leading to the expansion of the Navy and construction of "The Modernized Fleet" by 1005 C.S.

The Canterlot-class armored cruisers would be a fine example of a late 19th Century warship on Earth. On Equus, they're the first of their kind. Boasting impressive firepower far in excess of similar ships of their Earthly era, the Canterlot-class vessels massively overmatch most of Tirek's cruiser types, most of which - until the sighting of the Steel Monitor at Alamo Island - had been mutant wooden ships-of-the-line. Even Tirek's more modern vessels, up to his Battleships and Gun Monitors, are inferior to this class in some way, as late as 1005 C.S., when the Canterlot-class was supplimented by the Konzas-class Heavy Cruisers.

Sharing almost the same hull as the Canterlot-class cruisers, the Manehattan-class Protected Scout Cruisers have an even shallower draft when underloaded. Fast vessels, Torpedoes were added to them soon after completion, to give them a knock-out punch against ships above their weight class. When the "Modernized Fleet" was launched in 1005 C.S., modifications were done to the Manehattan-class to give it some anti-aircraft ability, which called for removing the side-by-side single 95mm guns, and replacing them with quadruple 25mm AAA guns.

When the wooden Bellerophons were determined to be "not nearly enough ship," it was decided to move forward with the Steel Fleet. The first four Bellerophon II's are "the same ships" as the wooden screw frigates, in that they were brought into drydock and modified piece-by-piece into this form, essentially creating all new vessels. Wood from the Screw Frigates was used to furnish and deck the Steel ones, and excess wood was used on other vessels as needed. All ships after F-04, are entirely new construction. The Bellerophons were later supplimented by the Heroes-class Modern Fleet Frigates.

The diminutive Alpha Corvettes were designed to provide both close protection for larger vessels, and as a method to insert special operations teams (of COURSE this mainly consisting of General K and his staff). The torpedo armament gives them some ability to fight off or damage larger vessels, while the 95mm main guns can take any of their peers or rival vessel types. The original HMS Alarm was piece-by-piece modified into this vessel type and served as the class prototype, after its caputre from Tirek at Carousel Island, and after the Steel Fleet Program was initiated. These vessels are not without controversy, as an incident involving four of them led to a Court Martial after the Battle of Fillydelphia Harbor.
