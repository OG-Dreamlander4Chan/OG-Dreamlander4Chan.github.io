Kingdom of New Equestria: Warships



The New Equestrian Royal Navy suffers from the same rot, lack of ponypower, and funding issues as the rest of the New Equestrian Armed Forces. However, they have insanely powerful units and global reach, with the Global Prompt Strike missile system.
New Equestria still uses the same system they did during the Great Tirek War - all ships receive a prefix code and are titled "HMS" for "Her Majesty's Ship."

The most advanced ship ever made, the Wishing Star represents the pinnacle of naval strike technology, with a defensive and self-protection suite capable of tracking every incoming object as small as a bird, out to twice the effective range of the defensive system's response time, giving it it more than enough time to react to any incoming threat. However, the only unit was mothballed in 2040 C.S. and its sister ship sits at 45% complete and laid up, unfinished.

Two more were planned, but were never constructed. The hullform for the Legends-class battleships is identical, and provides a very high speed and low friction across the length of the hull.

The Sargasso Sea is considered the "more punchy" of the two vessels, given its larger anti-ship armament. They were made from relatively identical hull forms, with stretches and modifications. It lacks GPS missile capability, but is usually armed with primarily offensive weapons, leaving the job of defense to other vessels.

The Guided Missile Destroyers of the fleet have the ability to act either defesnively or offensively as independent units. Unlike the other vessels in the fleet, the ship was designed to maximize stealth, so it can act as a silent protector of the fleet, or as a powerful strike tool. It does, however, pale in comparison to the fast atack abiltiy of the fleet's frigates. The unique 155mm AGS has an insane range, approaching that of short range cruise missiles, and with a similar payload. The trade-off is an inability to track moving targets as easily, makign it best used on shore targets... over a hundred kilometers inland.
