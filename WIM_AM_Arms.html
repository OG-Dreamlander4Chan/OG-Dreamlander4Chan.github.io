Kingdom of Ambleion: Firearms



Perhaps not coming as a surprise, but the "tinker culture" of Ambleion jumped on the idea of creating firearms rather quickly. From backyard hobbyists, to "two blokes in a shed," many designs were submitted as soon as word came that Ambelion was merely going to attend the NEIGHTO Conference. Within 18 hours of the Technical Data Package for the Zhukov arriving in Ambleion, 7 different "hobbyist" variants were officially submitted to the Ambleion War Information Office. In the end, most arms would be produced at Henfield Lock, but several hobbyist designs would be produced for The Home Guard (the Ambleion Militia system) local units, resulting in Ambleion having the second most Zhukovs in inventory, and having at least three dozen small scale ammunition plants throughout the islands, some more complete than others.

Interestingly enough, this also led to the Ambleion Charter of Rights having an alteration made in 1004 C.S. that enumerated the responsibility of every Ambleionian to maintain arms for defense of the Kingdom. What was originally a responsiblity for the Head of the Household to maintain a "Blade, Bow, Arrows, Quiver and Accoutriments for 10 days campaign" turned into a responsibility to "maintain arms of modern design at no less than one weapon per responsible party, and no less than 120 rounds of ammunition, plus basic carrying equipment and rations for five days field duty."

MANUFACTURER: Sauer Industrie Gesellschaft und Waffenfabrik (Pistole 291)
Vickers-Armstrongs Ltd.
CALIBER: 9x23mm NEIGHTO
CAPACITY: 10 + 1 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 50 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 114mm/4.5 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 216mm/8.5 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 158mm/6.22 inches
TOTAL MASS: 2.2lbs/1kg
The SIGW P291 is exclusively issued to NCO's of the Royal Horse Guards Regiment as a sidearm. As the RHGR is both the oldest and "most elite" line formation in the Ambleonian Army, they're often issued special armament in the first place. The design is of Germane origin, from the SIGW company, but the L11 is manufactured by Vickers-Armstrongs without alteration, with imported button-rifled barrels. On the larger side for a hoofgun, the L11 is also rather heavy, but well regarded for its precision and tight parts fitment, leading to excellent reliability.

L7 Assault Rifle
WEAPON TYPE: Assault Rifle
MANUFACTURER: Birmingham Small Arms Company (BSA-2)
CALIBER: 7x40mm NEIGHTO
CAPACITY: 20/30 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 500 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 420mm/16.5 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 820mm/32.28 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 206mm/8.1 inches
TOTAL MASS: 8.72lbs/3.95kg
Ambleion's L7 Assault Rifle is a very conventionally laid-out rifle based on the last major Crossbow design used by the Ambleonian Army. The wood stock splits into three pieces to allow easier access to the internals, riding on steel rails. The casings eject out of the top of the weapon, and to the back-right. The bolt cocking lever is in the centre-rear, like the Sherman, that can be bitten and pulled back (again, like the Sherman). The L7 is unique in that it's the only major assault rifle fielded by a major NEIGHTO partner that was designed to have an optical sight installed by default. This 1.1x optical sight allows the user to hit targets at slightly longer ranges than other standard assault rifles, and as a result, Ambleonian soldiers receive extra marksmanship training to take advantage of this.
L9 Sharpshooter Rifle
WEAPON TYPE: Assault Rifle
MANUFACTURER: Birmingham Small Arms Company (BSA-2P)
CALIBER: 7x40mm NEIGHTO
CAPACITY: 20/30 Round Detachable Box Magazine
EFFECTIVE RANGE: 750 meters
BARREL LENGTH: 420mm/16.5 inches
OVERALL LENGTH: 820mm/32.28 inches
OVERALL HEIGHT: 206mm/8.1 inches
TOTAL MASS: 9.1lbs/4.12kg
The L9 is an L7 with a Parker-Hail "Sparrow" Scope, a fixed 4.5x magnification scope that easily allows sharpshooters to reach out to over 700 meters. Ambleonian Snipers are trained harder than any other NEIGHTO partner nation, and are largely drawn from "bog hunters" from Scotland and other lowland areas, where crossbow hunting was common before the introduction of firearms.
