My Little Pony: Marking Warfare's Tale




Chapter 5: Battle of Cupcake Falls

Part 1: Reconnaissance

TIME: 11:37 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 12/18 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: 1km from Cupcake Falls, Erie, New Equestria


We were in position behind Windy's hoof-picked scouts. It was a Code Red, meaning, some kind of actual enemy had attacked the town. Cupcake Falls was an important transitory point between Erie and the old Erie Command area, and the North Atlantic Command. We got one radio message, and that was it.

What I had was what could be sent. Many ponies had at least one tour of three years militia service, and thanks to my intervention over 1,000 years ago, with the apocalypse, guns were common. Ponies had means to protect themselves. The problem was, MOST monsters were Umbral creatures, of formless mass and only vulnerable to magic.

Science was kinda fucked, if I'm being honest, here.

Whatever attacked Cupcake Falls, though, was not these. That was certain. That would be a Code Purple. Red meant things that bleed. The rare times in the last hundred years these mass attacks had happened, it was usually an overpopulation of carnivorous monsters from the woods seeking out ponies as prey out of desperation.

Again, this wasn't that.

Windy herself trotted up to me.

Windy: "We reconned." She smirked.

K: "Because a good leader, leads from the front." My lesson.

Windy: "You betcha.." She nodded to me, then frowned. "They're bipedal, and they're carrying guns. They've killed a few, but they have the rest hostage at the cafe. They're looting, right now. I'm not sure what they're planning, but they're making off with everything - I saw stacks of guns from the army outpost, crates of food, some sacks with Bits in them-"

K: "They're taking money?" I tilted my head.

Windy: "Only the coins." She shrugged. "I saw a few bills flying in the wind."

K: "You moved to paper currency." I looked at Sunny.

Sunny: "We kinda didn't have a lot of gold and silver after the apocalypse, Grandpa. The coins - 1, 5, 10, 25, 50 Nails, and 1 Bit coins are made of metal. 1's of copper, 5's of nickel, 10's, 25's, 50's and 1-Bits of silver." She nodded.

K: "Back in the day, the Bit didn't have a proper name for fractions, and trade was usually done as whole amounts." I shrugged. "What about the paper?"

Sunny: "2, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, and 500 Bits denominations." She nodded. "Silver Bit Certificates, redeemable for hard silver."

K: "Alright." I nodded. "As long as you don't go for that Fiat currency horse manure." I shrugged. "So, they're taking the coins. That means there's a civilization out there that conducts trade in precious metals as currency." I shrugged. "This just got a lot more complex." I looked to Windy. "Describe the OpFor. Height, weight, all that."

Windy: "Well, most of them are about my height, or around there. Say, 150 centimeters. They have yellow-green skin-"

K: "Troggles." I frowned. "By Luna's Mane."

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "What.. are those?" He tilted his head.

K: "Tirek's former elite shock troopers. Armed with better weapons than his standard forces, good at light infantry tactics, bad at hoof-to-claw." I shrugged. "They're as good or better than standard Infantry in our ranks... at range." I nodded. "They also employed sharpshooter weapons, grenade launchers, and light machine guns."

Windy: "WHAT are they doing HERE?!" She scowled.

K: "I have no idea." I shrugged. "We need to capture one." I nodded.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "Are we sure we can take them?" He looked concerned.

K: "We will try. I have every reason to think they'll execute the hostages when they're done. They did that before - indiscriminate killing of unarmed, non-resisting civilians, just because they were ponies." I shook my head. "Windy, your Rangers better be ready - you'll be on point." I nodded.

Windy: "We're ready, General." She smirked.

K: "Can you give me an idea of numbers?" I nodded to her.

Windy: "About a company. We saw 44 of them, that we could count." She nodded, and handed over three sets of field notes.

K: "Alright." I looked at the rest of the unit. "We barely outnumber them. No frontal assault - we need 3-to-1 superiority for that to be effective." I looked back to Windy. "Your best platoon, arm them with suppressors, I want them infiltrating by squad. Silent kills, work their way in until they can secure the main part of the village. I want them inflicting casualties. Once they're almost to the main part of the village, the rest of us move in." I pointed to Golf team. "You take the south, block them. Dig in good, we're going to try to push the enemy to you." Then to Dog Team. "You're going to move north and when I give the signal, advance with vehicles, use them and everything else as cover, and start pushing. The rest of the Rangers will be with you. The select platoon will form a wall and prevent them from fleeing east. Between our three forces, we should be able to take them out." I nodded.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "And the hostages..?" He squinted. "No way you're going to try and get them yourself. Sir."

K: "Yes way." I nodded. "Pipp, you're not trained in anything besides that camera, but I want you recording this. Not live streaming, recording, you can go live after we win."

Pipp: "Ooo.. my first War Report!" She smiled. "Fab. I have my first song idea ready, too."

K: "Misty, you stay with me at all times." I looked at her. "We don't have any magical options besides you, but if you see a chance to use magic to turn the tide, tell me before you use it, but use it." I said, softly. "Whatever you know, might save lives."

Misty: "I-I understand.. you'll protect me, right?" She looked me right in the eyes.

I paused for a moment.. looking back.. seeing hope, though timid, shining through. She wanted to help, but she wasn't ready to fight on her own.

K: "I promise, I'll protect you." I gently put my hand to her face, and caressed her cheek.

Really, you should never make promises like that in the battlefield, but sometimes, you have to. Either for the morale of the other party, or because you feel you need to say the words. It's a dangerous promise. Especially if you're not physically ready to fully carry that out.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "Forgive me, sir.. you... don't look like you're in shape to pull this off." He really did have a point, but he also didn't want to be presumptive.

K: "I'll have to be." I said, flatly. "You're counting on me." I smiled to him, a bit.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "..yes, sir." He gave a tiny smirk.

Sun finally understood. Why, a thousand years ago, ponies willingly followed me to the gates of Tartarus. Why they were so willing to fight at my side. Because, no matter what, wounded or not, weakened or not, I was determined to share the risk with them. They were not numbers, they were my troops. My. Little. Ponies.

I would never ask them to take a risk that I myself wasn't willing to take as well.

K: "I'm going to creep forwards and get one last look. After I do, I'll radio back and then we move in, according to the plan." I looked at each of them. "Ready?"

Windy: "Ready, sir." She nodded.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "Ready, sir." He nodded.

2LT Iron Bar: "We're ready, General." He smiled.

They were the first to see it. My mind was back - the body would follow eventually. Currently writing a check I knew my ass couldn't cash, I moved ahead by myself, lowering myself to the ground like an old man, and crawling forwards with my rifle in the crook of my elbows. Windy joined me, of course, to get back to her position.

Then, Misty did.

I looked back at her with a cocked brow, and she just shrugged at me, like, 'you told me to do this, right?' And I nodded, and slid along the ground. I knew my hips were going a bit too high, but my body just wasn't as flexible as it used to be. Damnable ass cheeks sticking up for all to see.

I finally got to the Ranger squad's position, and looked through a pair of binoculars I was handed.

Yep.. Troggles. Like the Gizmonks earlier, they seemed "stained" with some kind of dark energy, and their eyes glowed. It was all very strange.

Misty: "Hm." She scrunched her snootle and squinted. I loaned her the binoculars and she got a better look, before her jaw slowly dropped. "Ohhh.. they have the aura about them, I think.." She looked back to me. "The aura of Umbrals." She nodded. "Meaning, somehow, the Umbrals are linked to them." She shrugged. "I won't know more unless I can examine one."

K: "Right." I nodded. "As I said, we need a prisoner."

Windy: "We can handle that." She nodded. "My company is a bit short handed. We're down one squad's worth - our officers.." She cocked a brow at me.

K: "I was wondering." I nodded. "I'm guessing you're Regimental Sergeant-Major?"

Windy: "Yes, sir." She nodded.

K: "You'll do." I smirked. "Grab one." I nodded to a few stragglers sniffing around the edges of the settlement. "I'll cover." I said, accepting a suppressor from her and screwing it on.

Despite my age and body's condition, I was able to brace myself and steady myself for shooting. From what I could tell about these 6mm rounds, they had more power than 7mm NEIGHTO once had, so, they should down a Troggle in a shot or few.

Misty: "I can.. help with that." She snaked over to each of the Rangers in the Recon Squad, and doused their hooves with a potion who's smell I recognized from Zecora.

K: "That's Whisperstep Potion." I smirked. "Windy, trust me. You'll like that stuff. Be warned, it only lasts about fifteen minutes."

Windy: "We'll take it." She whispered. "Come on, Rangers." She slid through the underbrush with them.

I pulled gently on Misty's pant leg to get her to slide back some. Once she had, I felt more comfortable.

One of the Rangers pulled a hatchet when he got close to the two stragglers, and dove on one, cleaving its head open. The remaining five Rangers tackled the last Troggle and tied it up with zip-ties and duct tape, before pulling it back into the brush with them. Even the corpse was gone. No trace.

Expert work. I was glad to see that my Rangers were still the cream of the crop.

Once we had our Prisoner, I helped secure them more permanently with rope, and a blindfold. It was already gagged.

K: "Excellent work." I nodded. "Fall back, and prepare to advance with the main team." I nodded to Windy. C> "This is Alpha, prepare to engage." I said into the Mic.

Now, we attack.

[End of Part 1]

Part 2: Attack at High Noon

TIME: 12:00 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 12/18 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Cupcake Falls, Erie, New Equestria


We had a prisoner of war, and we had on-sight intelligence in real time. While New Equestria had a fairly small supply of them, Sun Sprinkles launched one of their micro-dones to watch the attack. Its TV camera would allow us to carefully watch the enemy.

K: "Wish we had these back during the War." I said, looking at the camera through an app on Misty's phone. "That reminds me, I need a working SIM card for my phone, or a new one that works here." I shrugged.

Pipp: "I'll help you pick one out! I know where to get THE. BEST. phone cases!" She beamed a grin.

K: "For the moment, Pipp, stay here in the command area with the vehicles. Zipp hasn't even started to train you, yet." I nodded.

Zipp: "TRUST me, Sis." She gave her a quick hug, though.

Pipp: "Alright. And while I do, I can practice my new song." She smirked.

K: "Hitch, Zipp, Sunny." I pointed to them, then myself. "Misty, I want you along, but please, stay next to me."

Misty: "No problem." She nodded.

Zipp: "This is right up my alley." She smirked. "AFSOC, remember?"

K: "Yeah, that's why you're taking charge if I fall." I nodded slowly to her. "Hitch, you might be hot stuff, but right now-"

Hitch: "No, no, I get it - she's more trained." He looked at her. "For noooow." And smirked.

Sunny: "Really, Hitch!" She gave him a smirk.

Zipp: "Mm-hmm! MMmmm-hmm!" She swished her flanks a bit, then got serious, giving her face a bit of grease stick, and snapping her suppressor on. "Ready, K."

We crept into the bushes, getting ready to thread the needle and charge for the Hostages. We needed to make sure they were safe. Once I was sure everyone was in position, I called it.

K: "Here we go." I gave a heavy sigh. C> "Alpha Actual - All Callsigns - Clear to ENGAGE!"

Gunfire started immediately. Some of it suppressed, some not. I waited a full thirty seconds, then forced myself to my feet. I nodded to the others, and moved forwards, forcing my body to move in ways it hadn't in a decade. My MIND was getting back, but my BODY was not there yet. I could feel the tension in my muscles, begging me to slow down, but my heart refused to let them get in the way.

I had to help those ponies. No matter what.

My "running" was best described as ungainly loping in gait, and coverage. But, that feel of adrenaline.. the rush.. even my aged body was starting to awaken. I cleared the space between two buildings, and as I moved to the street to cross it, I immediately, though slowly, pivoted to each side. To my left, a Troggle fell from another shooter. To my right, one was already aiming at me. Zipp put it down with a fast burst.

Really, watching the Troggle's body open up like a ripe melon... this 6mm stuff was pretty good. I forced myself up from the kneel I instinctively took, and rushed forwards. Blood pounding in my ears, I was getting tunnel vision, but I kept going. The doors to the cafe were just ahead, and I elected to jump as hard as I could, bash the doors open with my body, and land on the ground. This.. went about as well as expected, with me rather clumsily belly flopping to the ground.

I rolled left, fired a hyper burst, rolled right, fired two more, rolled to my belly, braced the rifle, and fired at the last Troggle behind the bar. I stayed prone as the rest of the team entered.

Hitch: "You okay, K?" He took a kneel to cover me.

K: "Yeah, give me a minute." I said, taking far longer to stand than even a raw recruit. Once I was up, though.. "Misty, Sunny, check the hostages."

Sunny: "On it." She tapped Misty, who reluctantly followed her.

K: "Hitch, Zipp, defensive positions, at the windows. We help the funnel." I said, forcing myself up, forwards, and to grab a thick table and clumsily throw it to the window, so I could take cover behind it. "I hope the rest of them are following the plan."

Zipp: "No kidding!" She broke the window next to her, and took position.

Hitch: "We got this." He nodded. "Easier than fighting Umbral shadow creatures." He shucked open his launcher, and slid a Flash-Bang shell in there.. "..just in case." He said.

The main body of the Troggles were moving down the main road in short order. They were in full retreat, and not even bothering to leapfrog or pepper pot their way through the streets.

They didn't talk, though, which was very odd - like most of Tirek's smarter goons, they usually gabbed while fighting. Usually to insult the enemy.

Hitch's instincts were dead-bang on, though, as two Troggles attempted to breach the cafe - his Flash-Bang at their feet made them retreat. Zipp switch to semi-auto and started taking pot shots. I did likewise - despite the adrenaline and ragged breathing, I was scoring 70% - I got 14 out of 20 hits. At least ONE skill of mine had only gotten better - I went shooting a lot before I was taken back here, so, my ability to hit targets was more a learned skill at this point, not a theory.

After a bit, the Chase team moved past us, and one member - the Medic - detached and came into the Cafe. They checked the hostages quickly, and departed.

Sunny: "The medic says no major injuries. They're just bucked up a bit." She reported.

K: "Roger that." I nodded. "Zipp, Hitch, sweep and clear the rest of the structure, just in case. Sunny, Misty, over here." I motioned.

Zipp: "You got it." She smirked. "Try'n keep up.. Sheriff!" She wiggled her brow at him, as they departed.

After a moment, I went for my canteen again. And I looked over at Sunny.

K: "You better lock him down before she steals him, Grand-daughter." I smirked.

Sunny: "What?!" She blinked. "N-No way! Hitch is just.. a friend!"

K: "Uh-huh." I said, sarcastically. "I was watching you two carefully. Don't forget, I -know- you Ponies. Sometimes better than you know yourselves." I shrugged. "Listen, Sunny, you both have feelings for each other. You need to express them." I said, with certainty.

Sunny: "..you think I haven't tried?" She tilted her head at me. "Hitch is.. popular, well liked.. admired.. he's been in a few relationships before." She looked down.

K: "And meanwhile, you waited." I nodded. "You're both friend-zoning each other because you're both thinking the other is the best match, and you can't stand the idea that the other doesn't think the same way." I nodded.

Misty: "Wow... how do you know all that?" She tilted her head to me.

K: "I've seen it happen before with other ponies." I stared straight at Sunny. "Now, I mean it, you both need to give each other a real chance." I nodded. "I'm going to tell him the same thing." And I suddenly leaned in to her. "The next thing you're going to say is 'No, Grandpa, don't do it!'"

Sunny: "No, Grandpa, don't do-" She blinked.

Why, yes, that WAS a Jojo reference! How nice of you to notice!

Misty: "How'd you do that?" She looked right at me.

K: "I know ponies. I know how you all think." I smirked. "And I know my family." I shrugged.

Windy: C> "Alpha, this is Ranger One." Her default callsign. "Mission accomplished, enemy defeated. No survivors. We have two injured."

K: C> "Roger that, Ranger One." I nodded, as Zipp and Hitch came back in. "Well, we did it." I gestured to them. C> "Alpha to call callsigns, meet up at the Cafe.. and someone fetch the POW and Pipp."

Windy: C> "On it, Alpha. Ranger one, out."

Well, that's that. We managed to take the town back. Now to figure out WHY this was happening in the first place.

[End of Part 2]

Part 3: Hold

TIME: 12:37 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 12/18 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Cupcake Falls, Erie, New Equestria


I had the prisoner brought into the cafe, which was now serving as my temporary HQ.

Troggles. I thought we defeated them a thousand years ago. They were part of Tirek's army, his field elites, and they were ordinarily well armed and equipped.

I was examining all the gear the Troggles had. While it showed signs of wear, it was clear it was all well made. Field cooking stove, canteens of steel, magazine pouches of ballistic nylon, lightly armored vests good for stopping shell splinters and shrapnel, the works. It looked like gear from the 1970's from Earth. Right down to the use of ALICE-like clips.

They had food on them. Most of it was clearly field ration quality. Dried goods. Most of it wasn't packaged. There was a small sack of potatoes on one of them, though, and two tins of something. Quart-sized, too.

K: "Hitch? Got a can opener?" I held out my hand.

Hitch: "Right here, K." He hoofed it over.

I opened the tin, and inside was exactly what I expected - mystery meat that resembled spam. I grabbed a fork, and dug in, tasting it, and shaking my head. Very salty. It was probably intended to be boiled into stew.

K: "Canned meat." I shrugged. "No labels on them, but the can is stamped with a drumstick-like design. The other one is stamped with.. an apple, I think?" I said, opening that one, too - this was tinned peaches. They were very sweet, soaked in corn syrup, clearly, but perfectly edible. "I don't like what this implies." I looked at my six new friends. "Conclusions." I pointed. "Pipp."

Pipp: "They don't have cell phones, but they do have radios." She shrugged. "They look like they made them, themselves, too. Like, to a pattern." She shook her head.

K: "Alright, and that implies?" I cocked a brow.

Pipp: "They have a factory! Or.. several? And radio towers!" She nodded.

K: "Very good. Hitch?" I looked to him, next.

Hitch: "They clearly have a well ordered military machine going on, here, and some kind of economy that uses money. They don't have many rations left, maybe two days, so, they probably attacked Cupcake Falls for supplies." He shrugged.

K: "Excellent. Sunny." I looked to her.

Sunny: "Well, the weapons are thousand-year-old designs again, but different. You called them Grants. That was the last major weapon type you made in the war. They're all relatively new with signs of use, but not over-use. They were also cleaned recently. And they have some tools with them. The ammo looks like old 7mm NEIGHTO." She shrugged.

K: "Very good. And the arsenal stamps match the rifles we got a couple days ago." I looked to Zipp, now.

Zipp: "Their tactics were really good, as good as you said they were, and they seemed to respond and react as we made moves. You're right, they're not dumb." She frowned. "This needs an investigation.."

K: "It does, it does, but we're looking for immediate answers." I looked to Izzy, who was bouncing, wanting to answer, but waiting her turn.

Izzy: "My turn!? Okay, okay, okay.. all the stuff they have is VEEERYY well made, like, factory made. But they also have all kinds of small tools and things with them. It's VERY well made, high quality, sure to last for ages type stuff. It's exactly what an infantry raiding party needs, and then some. They even had ROPE!" She paused a moment, like her train of thought derailed, but no, it merely switched tracks. She paced over to the POW, and pulled out a vial of glitter. "Watch this!" She shook it in its face, and it pulled back out of fear. "Iunno really what THAT means, but it means SOMETHING!"

K: "It sure does." I looked at Misty last.

Misty: "Uhm.." She shrugged very slightly. "..I think they're controlled magically by a higher power. Not totally, not every thought they have belongs to a hive consciousness, but like.. they can get orders from whomever it is. Instantly. And that entity can direct them around remotely." She walked close to the prisoner, and pointed her hoof at his eyes.

K: "I see.. excellent insight." I cocked a brow, then walked over to the prisoner. "Do you talk?"

Troggle: "Human of legend." It addressed me. "My master has come."

K: "Your master?" I asked, cocking a brow.

Troggle: "You cannot be allowed to win." It spoke, very well - I remembered them being very.. simple. "My master will stop you. We felt you arrive." He looked at the ponies. "You have nothing. You have no magic. You have only ponies. You will fail. Your friendship is meaningless in the face of total darkness."

K: "Very well spoke, and forthcoming with the threats. Is your master feeding you lines?" I cocked a brow, and set my fist on my hip.

Troggle: "He sees through my eyes. He speaks to my ears." He frowns.

K: "What is his name?" I leaned in.

Troggle: "Sendak." The Troggle says coldly..

K: "I have no idea who that is." I shrugged.

Troggle: "You soon will learn." He snarled.

Hitch: "What do we do with it?" He looked at me.

K: "Oh.. yeah.. this is the part everyone hates." I shrugged, coldly slid my Roc from its holster, raised it to head level with the beast and fired a single shot, evacuating its brains.

Everyone did, indeed, recoil.

Everyone, that is, but Misty.

Misty: "..it had to be done." She said, speaking up for me, unprompted. "Opaline told me the old ones were vicious, too - and this one's controlled somehow. It.. we couldn't save it."

Zipp and Hitch both looked at her with questioning gazes, like, how did she come to that conclusion? Sunny just frowned a bit, but looked at me with trust. Pipp was clearly reviled, and paced away, finding her happy place. Izzy went back to what the uninitiated would think is distracted staring - I could tell, the gears were turning in her head.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "Just... asking, sir.." He cleared his throat a bit.

K: "I'll answer before you have to ask." I nodded. "It's.. there's absolutely no way to save them. Life is sacred, but when you're dealing with puppets, the only thing we can do right now is render them a non-threat. That means killing them after we question them, if we must." I shrugged. "I don't like executing prisoners, either - it's unsavory and sad. But, until we can.. until we can get the magic back, and we figure out how to use it.. make it possible to break this control.. we have to protect ourselves." I looked at him. "That's why I'm doing it myself. I'd rather my soul bear the stains of the worst things war has to make you do. Not you." I lowered myself a bit to his eye level. "You don't.. ever.. deserve to have to bear that kind of pain or sin." I held my hand out.

He put his hoof in it, and we shook.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "I.. I'm sorry I ever doubted you, sir." He shook his head.

K: "You won't be the last." I nodded to him. "Pipp?" I looked over to her.

Pipp: "Not something song worthy." She stated, distracted.

K: "I'm sorry, Pipp." I shrugged a bit, and paced over to her as some of the MP's got rid of the body. "War is rough stuff, and sometimes, we have to do things we hate." I nodded to her. "I don't like it, either. But, I'll do whatever I have to, to save you, help you.. protect you."

Pipp: "Yeah." She gently nibbled her lower lip. "S-Sorry, I'm not used to this."

K: "You shouldn't have to be. But, this is what we have to do, to protect the ones we care about." I nodded.

Zipp: "See? He cares." She moved over to her sister. "He knows this is going to be rough." She put an arm over her. "Still want to come with?"

Pipp: "I have to." She said, blinking, looking from me to Zipp. "Come oooon, Zipp - my fans! They need to know. They need to know we're out here, fighting for all of them. Right?"

K: "Exactly." I smiled.

Zipp: "You're right, Sis." She smiled.

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "What's our next step, sir?" He nodded to me.

Windy: "...ah, that's going to have to be 'Try to hold the town' - we got incoming!" She pointed out the window.

Indeed. More Troggles were approaching from the north.. a company sized formation and change.

K: "Get to cover." I stated, and moved to one of the windows. "Windy, get your Rangers around the back of them, and give me some flanking fire. Take the IFV with you." I directed. "Sun, you and Iron Bar, spread out across the town - garrison the buildings."

Lt. Sun Sprinkles: "Got it." He moved off.

The rest of my team did as the others - we got to cover and made the Civilians get to the back of the building. It was a defensive action, now, and I expected surprises.

But.. I had an army again.

[End of Part 3]

Part 4: Defend

TIME: 14:02 NERST
DATE: Redemption Day 12/18 Septimus, 2054 C.S.
LOCATION: Cupcake Falls, Erie, New Equestria


We were in defensive positions, and had thoroughly garrisoned the buildings in Cupcake Falls. Before the Apocalypse, it had been a medium sized town, and there had been a lot of dead here when things went pear shaped a thousand years ago, leading to the outer edges of the town being abandoned. However, after so long a time, no real ruins remained. Modern Cupcake Falls had several large buildings near the former center of town, including a trading post, cafe and hotel, army outpost, post office, and a courthouse I was pretty sure was the same from 1,000 years ago. Besides that, there were some homes, and a small camp for transient ponies that wandered the world and never settled down much.

We were garrisoning all the major buildings, and were using whatever we could to board up the doors and windows. The army outpost had some permanent entrenchments - a couple of blockhouses of concrete, an observation tower with a machine gun, and all the buildings had concrete foundations. However, the blockhouses were all blown open in the initial attack, and one of the outpost buildings was flattened. The tower was still there, and we had a few soldiers up there - and while it was made of armored steel, it was still vulnerable to high powered AP rounds.

K: "I wish I had a proper machine gun." I frowned.

Hitch: "Just asking because I'm not familiar with the idea-"

K: "Volume fire that doesn't cease and can be used to pin down enemy forces while rifleponies take them out with precision fire, grenades, or whatever else. You use machine guns to fix the enemy in place." I gestured as I spoke. "They're also useful when the enemy comes in waves. 60 rounds on tap for each rifle is nice, but that's barely Squad Support Weapon or Automatic Riflepony quality. If you have a lot of them, that's not a problem, but if you're working with a small unit, you need a machine gun." I shrugged. "The one I saw on the boat? Eh." I frowned. "I'll need to see about designing a proper machine gun for us."

Sunny: "Uhm, Grandpa?" She tugged my sleeve. "I have a few sketches.. you know.." She gave me a bit of a coy, bashful look.

K: "As expected of my little girl." I pat her head. "I'll have a look when we're not about to get our flanks shot off."

She gave the same squee noise I first heard from Fluttershy, but later learned all ponies could do. It made me smile.

Hitch: "Here they come!" He harshly whispered.
