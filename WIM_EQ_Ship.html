Kingdom of Equestria: Warships (Royal Equestrian Navy)



Equestria, initially, did not have an armed Navy. The Royal Guard maintained a small fleet of small sailing vessels for search and rescue operations, and the Royal Yacht, a Schooner-sized vessel, for pleasure excursions. Constructing a fighting navy from the ground up, General K had to initially adapt to the shipbuilding capability of the kingdom as it existed. After the 2nd Attack on Canterlot, however, the Steel Fleet Program was pushed forwards, despite the technical limitations of existing shipbuilding industry. While steel ships could be made from the very beginning, they had no concept of how ship armor worked at the time, and could not have constructed a warship. Hence, an initial reduction in technological era to facilitate faster shipbuilding and allow for the warships themselves to be self-repairable in the field, instead of outright armored to withstand attacks.
Equestria has several major naval facilities, but the Navy itself is headquarted at Fillydephia Naval Yards, in Docksylvania. Other facilities include Baleston Naval Yards, Manehattan Naval Shipyards, and New Haven Naval Base. The Port of Mayaimi also has a dedicated set of docks for the Equestrian Navy that later became Saletine Naval Base.
Equestria uses the prefix "HMS" (Her Majesty's Ship) for its vessels. As Equestria is afforded the leading place in North Equus, all other nations with Royal governments use a prefix variant denoting their nation.

Built up from a captured wooden ship of the line, the Moonlight was a testbed for several technologies. It became the Flagship of the Equestrian fleet, soon after recommissioning. While it has not served as General K or NEIGHTO's only flagship, it was designed as a command-and-control vessel first, a fighting cruiser second. It has higher speed than the Canterlot-class, but thinner armor over the decks and superstructure, save the Bridge and Combat Information Center, which are very thickly armored, to prevent easy incapacitation. The ship is intended to be used as a command vessel in fleet operations, both strategically, and tactically. It is also capable of being used as a special operations support vessel. The ship was originally named Princess of the Moon by the late Admiral Saletine, who was killed in battle defending Fillydelphia Harbor from a sneak attack by Tirek's fleet.

The diminutive Alpha Corvettes were designed to provide both close protection for larger vessels, and as a method to insert special operations teams (of COURSE this mainly consisting of General K and his staff). Almost as large as the Yamato-class battleships of Neighpon, the Rainbow of Light class Fast Battleships represent the pinnacle of battleship technology. With a dozen 16-inch guns each firing what amounts to a Ford Escort packed with high explosives, and erasing an area the size of a tennis court when they hit, the weight-of-shell alone allows one Rainbow of Light to easily overmatch two of Tirek's smaller battleships. The impressive AAA armament is more than enough to counter Tirek's air threats as well. The only thing it lacks is anti-submarine protection, requiring it to be escorted at all times. However, in the open ocean, it can simply sail away from offending Subs with its impressive 34 knot top speed. With the HMS Moonlight retired from active service, the Rainbow of Light class ships were all outfitted to act as Force Flagships, with accomodations for General K and up to 60 staff, with their own private war room, quarters, and mess hall.

HMS Luna was severely damaged at the Battle of Fillydelphia Harbor. As a result, she required significant reconstruction. The decision was made to modify her, rather than restore her, to address some design limitations discovered after she was originally commissioned. This included upgrading from Mk. II to Mk. III 12-inch gun turrets, to addressed cramped conditions, deleting the 8-inch secondary armament in favor for long range 5-inch guns, partial replacement of her boiler arrangement, replacement of her flat-headed propellers for conical propellers, and replacement of the cramped "cone" superstructure with a modern rectangular one (with the uppermost level retained as a Flying Bridge). Eventually, Cadence would be similarly modified, but both Celestia and Shining Armor would be reclassified as Coast Patrol Battleships (PCB) Summer Solstice and Radiant Shield to free up the names. After modification, it was decided to reclassify them as Battlecruisers, not Battleships. Additionally, Luna was reconstructed to function as a Flagship, with a dedicated space near the Combat Information Center for General K, a staff of up to 30 in private quarters, and a planning room attached to the CIC itself. Cadence has this same section set aside for coordinating landing operations, with additional communications equipment.

A truly modern, fleet ready Light Cruiser, the Kichai-class represents the definitive structure of the Light Cruiser, with 12 Anti-Ship 6-inch guns, and 10 dual-purpose 5-inch guns. Its anti-aircraft compliment is also impressive, and it has both Scout Planes and boats. A very much "do-all" cruiser, its top speed makes it attractive for hit-and-run attacks, and it does have the ability to support a small beach landing. In every way, the Kichai-class is superior to its predecessors, in the Manehattan-class. The addition of gyroscopically stabilized torpedoes also means that the Kichai-class ships can snipe enemy vessels at longer ranges with more assurance of a clean hit.

"While their souls have passed into Celestia and Luna's grasp, they watch over us still" is the motto of the Heroes class ships. Each one named for Ponies killed in action during the war - which there is, sadly, no shortage of - they represent the burning desire of each fallen hero to protect those most important to them. The ships are an evolution of the Bellerophon-class in every way, with a greatly expanded arsenal. Torpedoes, depth charges, and a main battery equal to the secondary battery of a Light Cruiser, combined with good speed, make these nimble sea warriors equally good at Force Protection and hit-and-run strikes.

With the sinking of the Bellerophon at the Battle of Fillydelphia Harbor, the upcoming "Bravo" class was given the name. An evolution, actually, of the "Alpha" or "A-Class" Corvettes (which were all surplussed to coastal defense duties), the third installment of the legendary "Bellerophon" name sports a rather hefty anti-ship, and anti-sub armament, and decent anti-air protection for a ship its size, combined with a thin hull and a tight turning radius, making it an excellent ship to screen the fleet with, or act as an escort to larger vessels.
