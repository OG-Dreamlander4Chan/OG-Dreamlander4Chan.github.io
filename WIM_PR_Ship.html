Kingdom of Prance: Warships (Marine Royale)



The Prench Navy has a reputation and tradition almost as long as the Ambleonian Navy, and a strong rivalry with them. This being Equus, this has devolved into races, stealing each other's flags, uniform hats of officers, occasional food fights, and the rare outbreak of fisticuffs and outright ship theft in antiquity. Nothing serious has arisen in the last 70 years, and while the two services still see each other as rivals, the rival is very friendly, especially since the Entente Cordiale of 904 C.S.
Prance does not use a Ship Prefix like the Ambleonian and Equestrian Navies, so, while Equestria would list a ship as "HMS Canterlot," or Ambleion as "HMAS Howe," the Prench would simply say "Cruiser Loire" or just as "Loire." Prance does use its own Pendant/Pennant system, somewhat based on the Ambleonian version, but with different letters.

Very slightly larger than Inflexible, the Province-class has an unusual gun arrangement, where the main guns are focused forwards, intended to be used primarily in an end-on chase, which also reduces the ship's shilouette, making it harder to hit - at least in theory. The ships also boast an impressive compliment of boats, and a larger coal bunkerage, as well as a protected smoke stack that's armored up to 6-inch shells. The incredibly tall design allows the fleet to have a MUCH longer sight line than its competitors, but does leave the superstructure vulnerable to attack and detection right back.

Prench cruisers both use the same gun, the high-velocity 160mm. The Regalien-class are built as "gun cruisers" - that is, their main striking power comes from its gun armament. The 160mm shell is the largest that can be rapidly handled by a Pony, leading to as fast of a fire rate as possible. Combined, the 12 guns have 1344 shells in ready ammo, allowing them to stay in the fight for almost five hours of continuous firing.

The tiny Torpedo Boats of the Militares-class, while technically not intended to be ocean-going combatants, do well in the role, given how they were constructed similar to "racing schooners." They're one of the few vessels with torpedo tubes located below the water line, intended to strike deeper-draft vessels where they're weakest, by launching the ordnance below wave height. Interestingly, the only "superstructure" level is entirely circular, and has 4 single guns at the cardinal "corners" of the deck, essentially making one huge casemate.

The Jeanne d'Arc class are named for famous Prench historical heroic figures. They're also some of the largest Carriers in the NEIGHTO fleet. While they did use the Province-class Battleships as a starting point, they quickly diverged, significantly in design and layout. The four Prench carriers have a VERY large aircraft compliment, and only come in second to the Equestrian Cloudsdale-class carriers. French Naval Aviation is highly regarded as well, making the Prench carriers the new crown jewel of the Prench fleet.

An overhaul of the vessels was carried out soon after the Neighpon Campaign, and with the adoption of Aircraft, the Province-class Battleships now also function as combat air patrol-capable vessels. The gun calibers were increased, new turrets fitted, the casemates removed, and new dual purpose 5-inch guns (Equestrian 127mm guns in Prench turrets) replaced them. For closer range AAA defense, 55mm guns were developed, though the Prench discarded the idea of using 25mm cannons, fearing them too light for the task, given the deck space available on Prench vessels. They remain exceptionally tall, which provides them with greater gun sighting ability. The all-forward main armament and the increased speed make these "chaser battleships" - intended to be bow-on to the enemy at every opportunity. A new bow was even fitted to make them more hydrodynamic and permit the installation of Sonar. Two additional units were constructed, as well.

In addition, the ship carries two Scout-Fighers for air support.

Upgrades to the Fleuves-Class ships include removing the torpedoes and adding anti-sub mortars instead, plus the new bow with sonar, and the addition of a scout plane to spot enemy vessels and submarines at longer distances. Gun armament was altered, with three dual purpose 5-inch gun turrets added, for AAA support, along with 55mm Autocannon. Boat stowage was changed as well. All in all, the Prench Light Cruisers are a very unique take on the concept, combining scouting, good gun power, and decent AAA capability.
