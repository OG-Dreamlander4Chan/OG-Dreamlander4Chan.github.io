Kingdom of Equestria: Firearms



The Kingdom of Equestria, where K first arrived, benefitted from his experience and knowledge in manufacturing firearms first. The commercial entity, "Filly Arms Manufacturing Company" became the first manufacturers of weapons for the Equestrian military, starting in late 1001 C.S. In addition, Mr. Dandelion Filly, the company founder, having a special talent for precision machine work, immediately became fascinated with firearms, and created several of his own designs for sale commercially. While the initial Equestrian "Charter of Rights" guaranteed the right to self defense, it did not explain what was and was not permitted. Given the state of the War, also, no one cared.
Equestria opened several military arsenals throughout the War, the largest being in Appleloosa, Texas. The other two most major Arsenals were the Springfield Arsenal, in Springfield, Maresechusetts, and the Milan Army Ammunition Plant, in Milan, Tennessee. The Filly Arms Manufacturing Company also housed the Manehattan Army Special Weapons Center, and was located in Manehattan, Erie. A large number of prototype weapons were fitted out at the MASWC for field testing. While the facility was owned by a commercial entity, the personnel stationed at MASWC were all Equestrian Army, or Erie Militia logistics personnel.

The second iteration of the "Custer" line, the "Hickok" adds (or, rather, restores) a spurred hammer, mainly for looks, but also introduces a single action notch to the hammer assembly. The "Makepeace" series of revolvers are, to the "Friday" series, what a .38 Special Revolver would be to a .357 Magnum revolver. Unlike the "Custer" series, which has a hard time using shorter ammunition types in longer cylinders, the swing-out cylinders of the "Friday" and "Makepeace" series use a different style of spring catch, and as a result, the "Makepeace" series can safely chamber 10x18mm Pony Express as well, whereas a "Hickok" would not be able to load that caliber safely.

The "Earp" revolver is a further evolution of the "Custer" line. Chambered in the 10x30mm Pony Express Magnum, it has power very similar to, though slightly less, than the Earth .41 Magnum caliber (~800j from a 5.6-inch barrel). Filly Arms' Sagebrush Shotgun was produced before the war began in small numbers, with a custom model hand-made for Applejack with her Mark on it. Popular immediately among the civilians of Equestria and elsewhere, FAMC released the Technical Data Package for free, to every arms maker in nations Equestria built an alliance with, to provide a very basic, high powered weapon for anyone's use. Both inexpensive and reliable, the Sagebrush is one of the easiest weapons a non-Unicorn can use, as the catch to open the shotgun is easily reachable with teeth, the spent shells auto-eject, and most ponies can easily grab the rims of two shells at the same time in their teeth, for fast reloading. As with Shotguns on Earth, shells are produced for every possible need - birds, large game, and defense. The standard Equestrian Military load is equal to Earth #1 Buckshot, with 24 pellets.

The first weapon produced for the Equestrian military, an essentially slightly enlarged PA-63 pistol in almost the same caliber, the Lee pistol was produced in large numbers, and widely exported as War Aid. Once the NEIGHTO standardization agreements were made, and the 9x18mm round not accepted as NEIGHTO's standard pistol round, 50% of Lees in Equestria's national inventory were sold off for commercial sale (the funds of which were partly used to offset the cost of the war). Commercial Lees came in stainless steel and two-tone, while strictly Military-issue Lees were Parkerized. In the image seen here, the Lee on the top is a Mark II or Mark III. There is no outward difference between a Mark II and Mark III as the Mark III altered the internal trigger linkage to make the trigger pull more smooth. Mark I Lees are quite rare, as most Lees produced were Mark III's, which started production 24 days before the beginning of the War.
The Lee (and its commercial cousin, the "Custer") both suffered from a lack of raw terminal energy, despite the ammunition being based on especially hot Czechoslovakian 9x18mm "9-82" ammunition, and both had a low effective range as a result, not helped by the very small blade front and U-Notch rear sights. This lack of performance led to the development of the much improved 9x23mm NEIGHTO round for handgun and sub-machine gun use.

Produced just before the war began, the Zhukov was based on the STEN sub-machine gun from Earth, with the intent that "parts kits" be distributed faster than manufactured whole weapons, and assembled by local Militia units that were rapidly formed across the Kingdom, so that the main production of Shermans - initially only at Appleloosa Arsenal - could be reserved for the primary units of the Equestrian Army and Royal Guard. As a result, several thousand Parts Kits were sent out all over Equestria, and hand-assembled as intended. However, this also led to significant embellishment and substitution of certain cosmetic parts, including things like leather or linen wraps around the stocks, altered bolt and magazine release handles, or different gauges of steel used in the main body, or different springs. Thus, the statistics listed above only strictly apply to Zhukovs manufactured at MASWC or Appleloosa Arsenal, who both produced them strictly to-pattern. Even after the adoption of its replacement, the Ridgway, the Militia Zhukovs were largely retained by their respective units as reserve weapons.

The Sherman Rifle was Equestria's stalwart defender during the earlier days of the War, with production starting on the Mark II version five months before the war began. Until much better powder was available, only pistol powder formulas were available. As a result, the Sherman's 9x35mm rifle round is basically a double-length 9x18mm pistol round, with a roughly double-length bullet. The weapon has an effective range of 200 meters, though a maximum range of up to 600 meters with a magical scope (first produced by Rarity using hand-sourced gems). With non-magical users, longer range shots ARE possible, with training. The relatively poor performance of this more-or-less Magnum Pistol round (fairly close to the legendary 9x33mm/.357 Magnum round) led to further design refinement, and ultimately, the adoption of a whole new rifle platform and round later. Thousands were handed out as War Aid after adoption of its replacement, and even before so, that Equestria's allies could have some kind of weapon, along with the Technical Data Packages to manufacture more. These continued to be used by Militia until as late as War Day 850.

Pegasi air support, the only thing available for the first 1/3rd of the war, required a special rifle. The standard Sherman had no way to be secured to a Pegasi during flight. So, a special stock and grip module was adapted. Shaped like a triangle, with the narrow point towards the bottom of the grip, the struts on the side allow a Pegasi to lay their entire foreleg into the stock, and strap it in place, so they can securely hold the weapon with just one foreleg while in flight. While handy, development of the Sherman's replacement rendered this solution obsolete.

Combat shotguns aren't often used in Human warfare, but in Equestria, where most things are scaled to about 3/4ths of that of Earth, a Combat Shotgun with decent reach can be nearly as effective as a sub-machine gun, and certainly with more power. The Chaffee is deployed by Rangers, Engineers, and some Line Infantry formations. With Sabot Slugs, it has an effective range longer than a Zhukov, with slight armor penetration ability.
The mechanism of the Chaffee is quite unusual. The barrel from which the projectiles are fired, is on the bottom. The magazine is on the top. Because hand-feeding shells in a combat situation for non-Unicorns is very difficult, it was decided that an ejecting magazine with an automatic bolt catch would be the only effective solution. The magazine, once inserted all the way, will retract the bolt, and load a live shell into the chamber.

The amazing Pershing Machine Gun, a stalwart and shockingly long-lived addition to Equestria and, later, NEIGHTO arsenals, was originally conceived as a tripod-mounted, semi-fixed machine gun for defensive use only. However, it was quickly shown that an air cooled version would work just as well. Given its very tame rate of fire of only 480 rounds-per-minute, like the M1917 Browning on which it was spiritually based, barrel overheating isn't much of an issue. The Pershing was later modified to accept the NEIGHTO-standard 7x40mm round, and the same non-disintegrating metal linked belt of the Abrams/Merrill machine guns.

The Patton was never really up to the task demanded of it. A major reason lie in the locally "enhanced" powders - Equestrian chemists simply could not reformulate the existing gunpowder formula into an effective rifle powder. While the Patton did have better range than the Sherman, and slightly higher terminal ballistic performance (very similar to the Earth ".25-20 Winchester" caliber in both velocity and muzzle energy), it simply was not ready for Prime Time. It was very quickly replaced, and was never officially adopted. However! The rifle's design would go on to inspire two other primary rifles of major nations, and rearsenalled Pattons would be given as War Aid, mainly to Prance, who would adapt them to the 7mm NEIGHTO round.
