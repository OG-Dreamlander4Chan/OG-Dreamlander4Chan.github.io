Kingdom of Ambleion: Warships (Royal Ambleonian Navy)



The Royal Navy of Ambleion has a tradition stretching back hundreds of years. Given the relative peace of Equus, most of this tradition is just seakeeping, sailing, occasional rescues of ponies lost at sea, and some minor conflict with pirates, but never anything serious. At the time of Tirek's assault in 1002 C.S., not a sailor alive had seen anything approaching "serious" combat. However, the small Ambleonian fleet was renown for their prowess on the seas, and boasted an impressive record. The transition to a combat navy was not smooth, however, even though they had talented shipwrights, and regularly used steel hull vessels already. As with most Equian navies, they lacked officers with command experience, and it showed.
Ambleion uses the prefix "HMAS" for "Her Majesty's Ambleonian Ship" for each of their larger vessels with proper names. By tradition, Pendant/Pennant Numbers in the RAN do not include a letter prefix for Capital Ships - this was usually for larger sailing vessels, and by 969 C.S., for the steel hull vessels Prince of Wales-class patrol ships.

The massive Inflexible-class battleships are the largest vessels ever made in Ambleion. The second largest were the Queen of Thames Cruise Ships, at 101.2 meters. The first ships designed with weapons, the construction went smoothly, as the designers used the basic drawing plans of the Celestia-class Battleships, simply altering measurements where needed. The ships are fairly stout, and are armored enough to take hits from guns up to 13-inch. The Inflexible-class are right on the line of what would be considered a "Dreadnaught" owing to their 6-inch secondary battery.

The County-class cruisers are an up-scale of the Prince of Wales-class patrol ships, which were nearing the end of their service lives when the Tirek situation arose. The Prince of Wales-class had no armament, other than Ballista of various sizes. Redesigning the general shape of the Prince of Wales-class to accomodate gun turrets, the designers came up with a solid, well constructed Armored Cruiser, proof to guns up to 9-inch across "the vitals" (engine spaces, lower waterline compartments).

Packing enough firepower to tackle enemies above thier weight class, or simply to lay down a torpedo spread to permit escape, the Town-class are considerably lighter than the Inflexible-class and County-class, with light armor only proffed to guns of 5-inch and below across most of the ship's reinforced sections, though 6-inch over the vitals.

Not QUITE as fast as the Bird-class corvettes, these Frigates can still, in a relatively close range engagement, stay close enough behind at full steam to provide full covererage with thier main guns.

These small vessels are the fast attackers of the Ambleonian fleet battle line. Armed with three torpedo launchers, and the highest speed of any Amleonian ship, they can dart in, lay smoke, fire torpedoes, and withdraw very rapidly. The 530mm Torpedo Launchers each have one full reload, letting lightning strike twice. These were based on steel hulled "racing schooners" that were popular around the Dover area from 980 C.S. until the war.

Modifications to the Inflexible-class Battleships have turned them into fine Fast Battleships, but at the lower end of the speed scale for them. A massive overhaul of the ship's design was undertaken to add the new 15-inch guns and not leave the ship top-heavy, that called for carving off a full deck and adding a new "Atlantis-style" raked prow. Almost all the Casemate Guns were taken off, save two, that survived the modifications. Aircraft were added. New boilers and power plants were added as well. It's almost a brand new ship, and actually slightly lighter than before.

The County-class cruisers were almost entirely rebuilt, with new bows added, and equipment changed around. New main guns were added, though the turrets remained dual mounts in a single sleeve (meaning both guns elevate at the same time). New secondary guns, the Ambleonian 4.7-inch gun, were added, and AAA protection increased significantly. Speed also increased, though they are the same speed as their sub-class, owing to the weight of their main armament. Scout planes were also added, with a hangar added abaft the stack.

The Gloucester Sub-Class cruisers are an evolution of the original County/Kent-class cruisers. Slighlty larger, with one more main gun barrel, and a better secondary and AAA armament. It also has more Scout Planes, and slightly longer transit range. It's well armored, and can easily stand up to heavier units with its powerful 9.2-inch guns. The Ambleonian-developed 4.7-inch gun is considered the best secondary gun in NEIGHTO for anti-aircraft duties, better than the Equestrian 5-inch guns, though it isn't as good against ships.

Refit Town-class cruisers are good general purpose Light Cruisers, with both anti-ship guns and anti-air artillery in relative balance, with anti-sub weapons, and new Gyro-Stabilized Torpedoes. Their follow-on sub-class fills the complete bill of a "modern" light cruiser a bit better, as even with the refit, only one Scout Plane could be carried. The new 6-inch guns are much higher velocity than the old ones, and perform much better against ship armor, allowing the Town-class light cruisers to have some chance against even enemy Heavy Cruisers.

Modernized Family-class vessels have been redesignated as Destroyers, even though they're a bit short for the classification, owing to their heavy armament, and expanded range. Anti-Sub weapons were also added, and the torpedoes changed to an Ambleonian copy of the Equestrian Gyro-Stabilized Torpedo. Speed has been increased as well, and the ships are far more capable than before. Four additional units were constructed from the ground up to this modified design.
