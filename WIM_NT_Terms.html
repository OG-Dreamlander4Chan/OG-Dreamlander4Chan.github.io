Glossary of Military Terms



This page is directly addressed to the readers. Not everyone has the same educational background, and even within that, will think different things when certain terms are used. So, this page is dedicated to rounding up certain terms encountered in the story that are less likely to be understood, or who's definition might be a bit different depending on whom is asked.


Naval Terminology
TERM: DEFINITION
Capital Ship A warship that is a major combat vessel of some kind. This term covers all manner of Battleships, Battlecruisers, Armored Cruisers, and Heavy Cruisers. This term also covers Attack and Fleet Aircraft Carriers, but NOT Light or Escort Carriers.
Flag Ship The ship that the current fleet, division, armada, or other naval force commander - holding an Admiral's rank - is currently "flying their flag" on, or commanding from. In emergency situations, this may not be a Capital Ship.
Armor Proofing A declaration that the armor on a ship can withstand a hit from a naval gun the same caliber as the main guns on the vessel, and not be penetrated by it.
Full/Total Armor A declaration that the armor on a ship covers both the sides (hull) and top (internal citadel).
Attack Aircraft Carrier An extremely large aircraft carrier with a very large compliment of aircraft (~50).
Fleet Aircraft Carrier An aircraft carrier that is at least as large as a battleship (~140+ meters) with an aircraft compliment between 35 and 50.
Light Aircraft Carrier An aircraft carrier that is at least as large as a heavy cruiser (~120+ meters) with an aircraft compliment between 30 and 45.
Escort Aircraft Carrier An aircraft carrier that is at least as large as a light cruiser (~100+ meters) with an aircraft compliment of at least 25.
Fast Battleship A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 14 inches (355mm), a minimum of 8 main guns, fully proofed armor, and a top speed of at least 33 knots/61kph/38mph.
(Dreadnaught) Battleship A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 12 inches (355mm), a minimum of 8 main guns, fully proofed armor, and a top speed below 33 knots. It should not have a secondary gun larger in caliber than 6.1 inch (155mm).
Battlecruiser A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 10 inches (254mm), a minimum of 8 main guns, and a top speed of at least 30 knots/55.5kph/34.5mph.
Pocket Battleship A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 10 inches (254mm), no more than 8 main guns, and a top speed of at least 33 knots/61kph/38mph. It is not required for the armor to be fully proofed to its own main guns.
Pre-Dreadnaught Battleship A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 12 inches (355mm), a minimum of 8 main guns, fully proofed armor, and a top speed at or below 30 knots. In addition, it would have a set of secondary guns of at least 8 inch caliber (203mm) and no provision for major anti-aircraft weapons (20mm autocannon or larger).
Armored Cruiser A warship with a main gun caliber of at least 9 inches (228mm), at least 6 main guns, armor fully proofed to its own main guns or better that fully covers all important internal spaces from both sides and top, and (generally) a top speed below 33 knots. It should have no major anti-aircraft capability.
Heavy Cruiser A warship at least 125m long, with a main gun caliber of at least 7.8 inches (200mm), at least 6 main guns, armor fully proofed to its own main guns or better, and a top speed above 30 knots.
Protected Cruiser A warship at least 110m long, with a main gun caliber less than 8 inches (203mm), at least 6 main guns, armor fully proofed to its own main guns that DOES NOT fully cover all internal spaces, and a top speed above 28 knots/52kph/32mph.
Light Cruiser A warship at least 105m long, with a main gun caliber less than 7.8 inches (200mm), at least 6 main guns, and a top speed above 30 knots.
Scout Cruiser A warship at least 100m long, with a main gun caliber less than 7.8 inches (200mm), at least 6 main guns, armor not proofed to its own main guns that only covers the hull over the machine spaces and magazines, and a top speed above 28 knots/52kph/32mph.
Anti-Aircraft Cruiser A warship at least 105m long, who's main guns are entirely or primarily dual-purpose guns between 3 and 5.5 inch (76mm to 140mm), at least 6 main guns, and a top speed above 30 knots.
Destroyer A warship between 60 and 100 meters in length, with main guns not exceeding 6 inch caliber (155mm). A "Fleet Destroyer" would be at the largest end of the scale, or mount at least 8 main guns.
Frigate A warship between 50 and 80 meters in length, with main guns not exceeding 6 inch caliber (155mm). A "Fleet Frigate" would be at the largest end of the scale, mount at least 6 main guns, and have a transit range of at least 1000 nautical miles.
Corvette A warship under 60 meters in length, with main guns not exceeding 6 inch caliber (155mm). An "Ocean-Going Corvette" should have a transit range of at least 900 nautical miles.
