Roaman Empire: Warships (Marina del Regno)



Roam is very proud of their navy, and while not nearly as disciplined as the Germane fleet, their ships were always large, visually impressive, and even beautiful in their own way. A great deal of ceremony, pomp-and-circumstance, and dog-and-pony shows went along with this, despite Roam losing its last major war against the former Ottoman Empire. However, this has led the rest of the European nations with a bad impression of Roam's military - that of a "force of show-offs" instead of a "show of force." This reputation was quickly buried during the Neighpon Liberation Campaign.. unfortunately, along side the graves of many brave Roaman Legionis.
Roam uses the ship prefix "RNS" for Roaman Naval Ship. They do not use a hull classification system at all, and instead, ships are assigned Dock Numbers that are only ever used on internal reports, and not as an official part of a ship's designation (Equestria, by comparison, would list a ship like "HMS Canterlot, CA-01").

Compared to the Germane battleships, the Roaman battlecruisers are lighter, much faster (the fastest vessels in the NEIGHTO combined fleet) and both more nimble.. and prettier. A design flaw of the vessel has two of its main gun turrets amidships, instead of fore-and-aft as with the other NEIGHTO partners. While this does limit its ability to bring its full weight-of-shell against an enemy vessel, when it CAN, it has 16 total naval rifles it can fire, giving it an unexpectedly impressive broadside with an equally impressive range, for such a light caliber.

Recognizing that their battlecruisers were very limited, and needing a Carrier platform, the Roaman Navy modified Ceasar and Rex into Aircraft Carriers. The modification was simple enough - shave off the top, add a flight deck with elevators, empty out the first superstructure deck level, reuse the former Barbettes as munitions and fuel elevators from deeper in the ship, replace the older engines with faster and more powerful ones, and remove a layer of internal armor belt to save some weight. The result is these.

The modern fast battleship of the Roaman fleet, the Imperio-class ships are as refined as the Rainbow of Light-class and, while the armament is lighter, the guns have similar range. The Roaman 127mm dual purpose gun has very good range, and has a proximity fuse bursting charge for use against aircraft. Its armor is as thick or thicker than the Rainbow of Light-class, and it has a better turning radius, owing to its Double Reduction Geared Turbines. All ships of this class come outfitted to be used as Flagships, and have rather impressive dining facilities for official functions.

The need to protect against aircraft was felt before Tirek's first attempts to strike naval assets, and the Roaman fleet - after developing an aircraft carrier - realzied this. So, the Province-class scout cruisers were modified into AAA/Force Protection Cruisers, complete with the Roaman anti-submarine mortar system, and the addition of a hefty torpedo armament. The Leonardo GST is a copy of the Equestrian GST, the data package shared freely due to the war. The "Riccio" (Hedgehog) ASW Mortar is unique in that it only explodes when it comes in contact with a Submarine, unlike a depth charge, which explodes at a pre-set depth.

Vaguley based on the Italian cruisers, these relatitvely heavily armrored landing ships formed the backbone of the Italian transport fleet until the introduction of the larger Regimental Landing Ships much later in the war. They were very handy for the Mareterranian Sea and nearby areas, but their slow speed made them difficult to bring along with larger fleet opertaions. Nopony could deny their thick armor made them very hard to damage, and resulted in fewer troop casualties, even standing up to magical blasts, thanks to the enchanted Silver Maple used to back the steel armor. This same Silver Maple was used to create the various Stradiverius instruments. Interestingly, this class was the only one to use the single barrel 127mm gun turret as it was originally designed.
