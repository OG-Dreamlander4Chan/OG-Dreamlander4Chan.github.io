Trixie Lulamoon: Weekend Magician

Episode 6: Cover Band - CONCERT SPECIAL





January 17th, 1012 C.S.
8:00PM
Grand Amphitheatre Grounds - Main Stage

Trixie was dressed in a black version of her usual outfit - black singlet, black fishnets, black pleated female suit pants, black cape, black magician's hat, the last two all with red stars and planets decorated across them, and a quick splash of red and silver glitter across her face.

She strode out slowly, confidently, with her heels clicking on the stage floor, echoing, as the crowd fell silent.

Trixie: "LADIES AND GENTLECOLTS!" She yelled out, swinging the microphone close to her face. "YOU.. were expecting the Dazzlings, right?" She held the mic towards the crowd.

They cheered for their favorite band. She smiled, nodded, and held up her other hand, rotating it at the wrist to encourage them to hype it up a bit.

Trixie: "Welll..!" She brought the mic back close to her face. "Just.. one little problem."

There were boos. Lots of boos.

Trixie: "Settle down, settle down.." She made placating gestures towards the crowd. "..the PROBLEM is.." She stepped back a bit, and made a partial bow. "..they're TRAITORS! And were ARRESTED! For trying to hypnotize Princess Luna!" She pointed to the Princess in her balcony seat.

The crowd's mood was a bit negative, but not at the situation, more at the possibility that someone would endager the Princess. Chants started of "LU-NA! LU-NA! LU-NA!"

Trixie: "Calm down! Calm down..!" She tried to get the crowd under control, but they were starting to get really whipped up into a frenzy.

Luna: "BE STILL!!" She shouted from her balcony seat, louder than the speakers.

Everyone settled down.

Trixie: "But, don't worry!" She paced across the stage. "YOU paid for a BIG show, you're going to GET ONE!"

The crowd cheered.

Trixie: "Now, I know they just performed at the start of the night, but.. once again.. let me introduce.. MY LITTLE ROMANCE!"

Stage light shifted back towards the back part of the stage, illuminating Galloway Hay, Ray Trotto, Martingale Hay, and Flank Irons. A new song started tuning in.

Galloway Hay: "This song was written in twenty minutes.. by the mare that saved us." He leaned back and smiled at the crowd.

Trixie was shadowed in a light ring, so the light was not directly illuminating her. She was in shadow.

The firework lit up its fuse, and as the band vocalized, it ignited, and fired off, as the song continued to play. While the rocket was attached to a wire guidance system, Trixie herself was attached to a harness. The rocket fired, and arc'ed out of the stadium, seeming to carry Trixie off with it. In truth, as soon as it vanished into the darkness, it was released from its wire guidance, and Trixie simply let go.

Trixie was lowered to the very rim of the Ampitheatre's main crowd area, and got herself off of the crane and harness, as the rocket exploded into a huge firework burst.

Dusk: "You know, T.O.W. Missiles cost about half a million Bits, each, right?" He smirked as he helped her get detached, and onto the zip like to make her reappearance.

Trixie: "Yeah, but I'm worth it." She smirked. "The GREAT and POWERFUL Trixie.. cannot be stopped." As she got on to the zip like, she reached out with an arm, lasso'd him, and kissed him on the lips.

That was more than a friendly kiss.

She let go of him, and zip-lined to the back stage area, with the Amphitheatre lights carefully hiding her. She was behind set and stage architecture and curtains in a few moments.

Just as the song ended, another massive firework and, now, smoke burst in the center of the stage, timed with the lights dimming and hiding the band, Trixie reappeared.

The crowd erupted as she reappeared. Trixie, for the first time in her life, felt amazing. She felt adored. Loved. Like she ALWAYS SHOULD HAVE BEEN! She bowed, and kissed to the crowd a bit, grabbing the mic again.

Trixie: "Short intermission, everyone! THEN! COLORATURA!" She cheered for her, her newest friend.



8:24PM
Backstage


Coloratura: "This is going to be fantastic." She smiled, and hugged Trixie, who did a quick change into a slightly matching outfit to Coloratura.

Coloratura was in a dark purple gown with a lot of colored frills to it. Trixie switched to her other singlet, which matched the tone and was made of a cheaper version of the same kind of fabric, so that even under stage lights, they'd look almost identical. She kept her fishnet sleeves on, though, since Coloratura's arms were in fine mesh sleeves. She added a silver necklace to match Coloratura's silver-accented choker.

It was a complimentary match, meant to make Coloratura look the most important, but like they were part of a set.

Trixie: "For the first time in my life, Coloratura, I feel amazing." She smiled. "I even kissed my ex." She winked.

Coloratura: "Yeah.." She smiled, half-closing her eyes. "..you think maybe you deserve him, now?" She stabbed at her.

Trixie: "WH-WHAT?" She looked shocked.

Coloratura: "Come on - I've seen this before. You felt like shit, like you didn't deserve a fine stallion like him, so you acted like a piece of shit, to get him to break up with you. Right?" She tilted her head.

Trixie: "..y-yeah.." She slowly lowered her head.

Coloratura: "So? Stop. You deserve him - you always did. Take your shot." She smirked.

Trixie: "He's getting married!" She hissed. "G-Granted, the wedding was put off because of the recent events.. but.." She shrugged.

Coloratura: "Fuck that. Homewreck. He was yours first, wasn't he?" She smiled at her.

Trixie: "Y.. yeah! YEAH! He was! I deserve a real chance!" She puffed up her two traitors and put her fists on her hips.

Sven: "TWO MINUTES TO CURTAIN! MOVE YOUR ASSES, FILLIES!" He demanded.

Coloratura: "Think about it, Trixie. Seriously. You're beyond awesome. You deserve that chance - that REAL chance." She tugged her arm. "Let's go.." She gave Sven a falsely polite smile. "And earn Sven what he deserves."

Sven just smirked, confidently.


8:30PM
Main Stage

Cheers and applause as they came on stage. Trixie was way behind Coloratura, letting her take the bows as her fans thronged to the stage side, trying to get her to touch their hands.

Trixie hung back, smiling.

Finally, she got to her Piano, and Trixie took her place nearby to do the backing vocals.

Coloratura started tuning up, and suddenly leaned into the Mic. What she said, sent the crowd from excitement to confusion.

Coloratura: "I wrote something BEAUTIFUL for you all, tonight. But my MANAGER said it wouldn't SELL.." She smacked her lips, and looked towards the offstage area.

Sven.. went white. Faint. Like he knew something was up. But, he couldn't stop this, even if he wanted to. Trixie had the crowd's favor, and interrupting both her and Coloratura would be a disaster for the brand.

The Brand that he'd WORKED SO FUCKING HARD TO MAKE AND KEEP FAMOUS.

His eyes burned lasers, as though he was watching someone soak all of his possessions in gasoline, and stood next to them with a lit match.

Coloratura: "So, I have something ELSE for you, instead!" She smiled, and nodded to Trixie.
